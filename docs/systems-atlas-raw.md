## ARCHITECTURE SUMMARY
Why the same bugs keep recurring: the codebase has no single authority for the two things a living-world game mutates most — WHERE things are, and WHEN state is written — and its many async/streamed seams each re-implement the same fragile bookkeeping by hand. Fixes have been per-symptom (bump an offset, add a serial, re-sync one marker) rather than removing the structural generator, so each family reappears at the next playtest angle. The fix-comment archaeology proves it: RuntimeBuildingBuilder carries the same 'pencereler yok' window bug 3x, the sim<->visual field split is re-reconciled per prop, and the request-serial idiom is copy-pasted across four dialog files.\n\nTOP-3 STRUCTURAL REFORMS THAT KILL THE MOST FUTURE BUGS:\n\n1. ONE SPATIAL AUTHORITY (kills families b + d, and the pile-up half of a). Establish a single rule: a thing's visual transform is a pure projection of its sim position — visual = BillboardOrigin(sim cell). Delete the parallel polar/ring cosmetic geometry (ARCHITECTURE_GAPS sec4). Add two invariants to the proof harness: (i) every rendered actor/plant/marker's world XZ equals its sim projection within epsilon; (ii) a containment/occlusion assertion (bounds-vs-solid + camera-hemisphere raycast) so a pane inside a wall or a body below the waterline fails CI, not the player. Placement helpers take a surface+normal, never a guessed literal offset.\n\n2. SINGLE-WRITER-PER-FIELD AT A DECLARED CADENCE (kills family c, hardens the determinism gate). Extend the existing WorldTickRegistry/WorldTickComposer bands into an ownership registry: each mutable field (position, target, needs) has exactly ONE writer system at ONE cadence; a second writer is a build/test failure (ScheduleSystem's every-tick guard pull vs hourly pursuit would have failed this). Make boundary-stamped catch-up (already the composer's convention) mandatory, and grow the seed-divergence replay gate to assert identical event logs across varied frame deltas per field.\n\n3. TYPED SEAM PRIMITIVES + GOLDEN COVERAGE FOR EVERY STREAM AND MAPPER (kills family f, most of g, and fences e). Replace the four hand-rolled dialog serials with one reusable latest-wins stream primitive (serial + dedupe + flush-boundary) used by dialog, speech, and LLM parse. Require a golden round-trip test for EVERY save-mapper field (extending the digest-roundtrip gate) so a dropped Home/DayAnchor fails CI. Provide one bias-free seed-persisting RNG facade and checked/clamped economy counters, banning raw '% n' and unchecked int math. Add a banned-symbols analyzer for legacy UnityEngine.Input (fencing family e to the single EmberInput seam) and for Unity fake-null misuse.

## (a) Single-point / single-truth designs [Sim + Presentation (worldgen, HUD, quest, map, visual tuning)]
DATA: One hardcoded location or one value that the whole system funnels through, with no plurality and no fallback. Canonical case: every hungry actor targets the SITE CENTER cell (the plaza) as the ONLY eat location; the player also spawns on the plaza, so the crowd collapses onto the player by arithmetic, not by design (ARCHITECTURE_GAPS.md sec1; ScheduleSystem.cs:94 'herkes town merkezinde'; Worldgen.Npcs hash-spread). Same shape elsewhere: the world map had ONE pin, the player ring (WorldMapView.cs:92 'markerlar dogru yerde degil'); the intro story lived in ONE log line, invisible (DomainSimulationAdapter.MainQuest.cs:7); class/level/XP were mock-only on ONE sheet (CombatHud.cs:174).
FLOW: Because there is exactly one sink, any pressure on the system (55+ actors, a dozen markers, a growing chronicle) all resolves to the same cell/pin/value and visibly piles up. The R2 saga (commit c3cb82de) is the tuning-time twin: a single pale-field constant was guessed blind and missed 16 times because there was no probe to read the ONE real value back.

## (c) Cadence writer conflicts [Simulation tick composition (multiple systems, different tick rates, same state)]
DATA: The same actor/world state is written by more than one system running at DIFFERENT cadences, and the faster writer silently wins. Flagship: WitnessResponse/predation move a pursuer ~1 cell PER HOUR, while ScheduleSystem drags the guard back to its post EVERY TICK (~60x faster) — so pursuit is arithmetically erased and 'guard chases' never renders (ARCHITECTURE_GAPS.md sec2; ScheduleSystem.cs:57-61 documents the same class for Enemies and exempts them, but forgot guards). Same family: walking is PerTick but eating resolves HOURLY, so actors reach the table in seconds then stand a full game-hour (sec1).
FLOW: Every mover writes actor.position/target on its own schedule with no declared ownership, so the highest-frequency writer overwrites the others between their updates. Timestamp variant: catch-up events were stamped at POST-advance time instead of the cadence boundary, producing off-by-one event-log drift when >1 boundary is crossed per Advance (WorldTickComposer.cs:245-260, ninth-pass A-P2; NeedConsumptionSystem.cs:29 catchup contract; EmberTickDriver.cs:30 counter alignment, PR#185).

## (d) Buried / occluded geometry arithmetic [Presentation (RuntimeBuildingBuilder, WorldDirector, geo)]
DATA: Procedural props are placed by MAGIC LITERAL OFFSETS with no occlusion/containment check, so they end up inside walls, underground, or overlapping. The canonical repeat: window panes placed with a 0.03 offset sat INSIDE the wall and were reported missing three separate times (RuntimeBuildingBuilder.cs:196 'LIVE BUG 3x pencereler yok'; earlier attempts at :182 and :189). Siblings: the L-wing offset 0.62 pushed a SOLID collidered cube through the room, reading as a doorless second room (RuntimeBuildingBuilder.cs:93 AUTOPSY; ARCHITECTURE_GAPS.md sec5); two building cubes overlapped until given distinct lateral slots (:242); the doorway top was open until a lintel closed it (:333); sleepers lay ON the road instead of IN the bed (WorldProjection.cs:96); ports sat below the waterline (commit 170fa8f4).
FLOW: Each offset is hand-tuned against ONE camera angle, ships, and the next playtest angle reveals the clip. Fixes bump the literal (0.03 -> outward, 0.62 -> 0.78) rather than asserting the geometric relationship, so the same class recurs on the next prop.
FILES: Assets/Scripts/Presentation/Ember/WorldDirector/RuntimeBuildingBuilder.cs:93,182,189,196,242,333; Assets/Scripts/Presentation/Ember/Adapters/DomainSimulationAdapter.WorldProjection.cs:96; Assets/Scripts/Presentation/Ember/WorldDirector/WorldSceneDirector.cs:229; Docs/ARCHITECTURE_GAPS.md sec5; commits 6953e3ac, 170fa8f4
LESSONS: Placement-by-literal has no notion of 'is this visible / outside the solid / above ground', so occlusion bugs are invariant under re-tuning. ## ARCHITECTURE SUMMARY
LAYERING (strict, enforced by asmdef and audited): Domain (pure data — WorldState, stores, GameTime, records; no Unity, no I/O, no RNG side effects) -> Simulation (deterministic systems that mutate Domain; no Unity) -> Data (pure DTO save mappers) -> Presentation (Unity; owns the real-time EmberTickDriver that calls WorldTickComposer.Advance). The rule the whole core is built to protect is DETERMINISTIC REPLAY: same seed + same tick sequence -> byte-identical WorldState and event log.

THE GATE CONTRACT (the determinism spine): a Presentation driver ticks at ~10Hz, but sim work is gated into three cadence bands — PerTick / Hourly / Daily — by WorldTickComposer, sorted by (Cadence, Order, Ordinal id) in WorldTickRegistry. MinutesPerTick (EmberRuntimeOptions) is the single source of truth; TicksPerHour/TicksPerDay derive from it. The catch-up contract is the load-bearing invariant: hourly/daily crossings are computed by integer division and each crossing runs stamped at its exact BOUNDARY GameTime, never at post-advance world.Time — so a one-day advance and a thirty-day advance produce identical logs. RNG is deterministic XorShiftRng seeded off the boundary stamp XOR actor ids; stores enumerate in canonical/insertion order; the cascade/shortage/history steps are deliberately STATELESS instances so nothing leaks between same-seed runs in the shared registry.

WHERE THE CONTRACT IS VIOLATED OR STRAINED: (1) TIME/STAMP HAZARD — TimeStep (PerTick, first) advances world.Time before the hourly/daily loops, so any system reading world.Time instead of context.Stamp breaks catch-up determinism; QuestSystem and CompanionService.TryRecruit/Dismiss still stamp events with world.Time, and QuestSystem dedups via a full-log string scan every hour. (2) STATEFULNESS AGAINST THE STATELESS-STEP LESSON — JobAssignmentSystem carries mutable _activeOrders/_completedExecutionCounts that are neither in WorldState nor written by the canonical save mapper, so in-flight recipes are lost on save/load and can leak if the instance is reused. (3) DUPLICATE LOGIC — two shortage detectors (stateful ShortageDetector unused; ShortageResponseSystem re-detecting with a different hardcoded threshold and re-emitting the same event kind) and two harvest implementations (inline HarvestStep vs orphaned HarvestSystem) with divergent semantics. (4) ORPHANED SUBSYSTEM — a correct deterministic A* (GridPathfinder/PathfindingSystem) exists but is not registered; all live movement uses ScheduleSystem's naive greedy Chebyshev stepping with no obstacle awareness. (5) SPLIT-BRAIN SAVE — WorldSaveMapper is only half the save (recipeWorkOrders intentionally left to the Presentation bridge), and the DTO tree / CopyFrom / EnsureInvariants are three hand-synced field lists (only CopyFrom is reflection-tested).

CADENCE-WRITER CONFLICTS (multiple systems writing one field at different rates): Actor.Position is written by ScheduleStep (PerTick), CompanionFollowStep (PerTick 21, ordered after schedule 20 specifically to 'own the tile'), and then Hourly PredationStep/WitnessStep/CompanionGuardStep — so an actor's final position each Advance is whichever step touched it last, with no owner. Actor.Needs is raised by NeedsStep (Hourly 30) and lowered by ConsumptionStep (Hourly 35) in a deliberately ordered same-hour raise-then-eat. Stockpile counts are mutated by HarvestStep (Daily), NeedConsumptionSystem eat (Hourly), CaravanStep (Daily), RuntimeHistorySystem caravan_surge (monthly), and TradeService — with the Daily price recompute reading whatever the interleave left. Faction reputation has two ordered writers (drift/chronicle then decay-last). 

SINGLE-POINT / GLOBAL-MIRROR DESIGNS: one flat WorldState god-object is the entire sim; one never-trimmed WorldEventLog is the whole audit trail (the ~1GB-by-day-90 memory leak, mitigated only downstream by summary events and depth-capped scans, not by bounding the log); the 'food spot' is every settlement's GEOMETRIC CENTRE rather than the actual larder cell (today's NPC-eating-convergence root cause — all hungry actors path to and cluster at the town midpoint); 'wheat' is a hardcoded universal food staple; the monthly grain surge dumps into Stockpiles[0]; job outputs mint into the single PlayerInventory; TavernCell is one communal lunch spot. Net: the composer/registry cadence machinery and the boundary-stamp catch-up contract are genuinely rigorous, but they sit on top of a shared-mutable god-object whose fields have no single writer, and several correct subsystems (A* pathfinding, stateful shortage detector, Phase-5 harvest) are bypassed by simpler inline logic in the live tick.

## NeedsSystem / NeedMoodEvaluator [Simulation (Living)]
DATA: ActorNeeds (immutable struct: Hunger/Fatigue/Thirst NeedValue 0..100). NeedsSystem holds only a NeedMoodEvaluator. Rate constants: HungerIncreasePerTick=8, FatigueIncreasePerTick=6, ThirstIncreasePerTick=5 (sized 'per game hour', re-balanced from the +20/+15/+10 ratchet era). NeedMoodEvaluator derives mood = Neutral - (hunger+fatigue+thirst)/3.
FLOW: NeedsStep (Hourly, order 30): for each alive actor ApplyNeeds(TickNeeds) which raises all three needs, then RecomputeMood. Emits ONE summary NeedChanged per hourly crossing (anchored on the first actor) instead of per-actor to stop event-log blowup. FILES: Assets/Scripts/Simulation/Living/NeedsSystem.cs, NeedMoodEvaluator.cs; Assets/Scripts/Domain/Actors/ActorNeeds.cs, NeedValue.cs, ActorMood.cs, NeedKind.cs
LESSONS: NeedsSystem is a one-way RAISE; it must be paired with ConsumptionStep in the same hourly band (30 then 35) or every actor ratchets into the job-refusal gate (>=80 hunger) with food rotting in stockpiles — the original 'CAN SUYU H1' root cause. The per-actor NeedChanged event was the documented source of ~900 events/game-hour, ~2M events / ~1GB heap by day 90 and the 1.4s Gen2 GC 'slow tick' spikes; collapsing to one summary event per crossing was the fix. Corpses are skipped (dead actors don't hunger).

## NeedConsumptionSystem / NeedRecoverySystem [Simulation (Living)]
DATA: NeedConsumptionSystem (stateless): HungerEatThreshold=55, EatReachCells=2, MealHungerFloor=5, MealThirstRecovery=40, NightSleepFatigueRecovery=40, night 22:00-06:00. FoodSpots(world) is fed to ScheduleStep every tick so actors walk toward their nearest larder. NeedRecoverySystem.EatMeal/Sleep is a separate player/action path (consumes inventory, emits NeedChanged trace).

LESSONS: SINGLE-POINT FOOD SPOT BUG (memory obs 18813, dated today): FoodSpots/WithinReach define the eating location as the settlement GEOMETRIC CENTRE ((MinBound+MaxBound)/2), NOT the actual stockpile/table cell — so all hungry actors path to and cluster at the town centre and eating only works within 2 cells of that midpoint, regardless of where the larder really is. Multi-settlement worlds previously routed everyone to the globally-first pile (fixed to nearest-of-many, but still centre-based). ## ScheduleSystem [Simulation (Living)]
DATA: Stateless utility selector. Constants WorkStartHour=6, WorkEndHour=20, WorkScore=55, IdleScore=35, NightRestBonus=25. A fixed 25-cell Chebyshev-spiral SeatOffsets table (5x5 around the food spot, all within EatReachCells=2) fans diners out to distinct cells.
FLOW: ScheduleStep (PerTick, order 20): for each alive actor, ChooseTarget scores eat (hunger value if >=55 and a food spot exists, else -1) / rest (fatigue + night bonus) / work (WorkScore if work hour and assigned) / idle, tie order eat>rest>work>idle; guards & pinned lair-enemies use ClassicTarget (home off-shift / worksite or day-anchor on-shift). Then StepToward moves ONE Chebyshev tile toward the winner. Each actor gets a stable seatOrdinal (insertion order) so shared destinations spread across seats.
FILES: Assets/Scripts/Simulation/Living/ScheduleSystem.cs
LESSONS: The midday tavern crowd EMERGES from rising hunger rather than a hardcoded window — the V1 'choreography, not behavior' lunch window was the canonical audit anti-example. But movement is a NAIVE greedy Chebyshev step (Math.Sign per axis) with NO obstacle awareness — the real A* GridPathfinder/PathfindingSystem is NOT wired in here, so NPCs walk through/around nothing and can rubber-band. CADENCE CONFLICT: this PerTick position write is immediately contestable by Hourly predation/witness/companion-guard steps that also MoveTo the same actor within one Advance; CompanionFollowStep is deliberately ordered 21 (after 20) to 'own the tile' — evidence the position field has no single writer.

## Cascade systems: Predation / Witness / Companion [Simulation (Living)]
DATA: All STATELESS step instances (everything derived from world+stamp). PredationSystem: HuntRadius=6, StrikeReach=2. WitnessResponseSystem: WitnessRadius=8, ResponseRadius=12, scan depth-cap 4096. CompanionSystem/CompanionService: MaxCompanions=2, RecruitReachCells=3, HeelCells=1, GuardReachCells=2; membership is WorldState.CompanionIds (ulong list), companions stay ordinary civilians with their roles/sprites/ActorMemory.
FLOW: PredationStep (Hourly, 40): each Enemy actor — a Guard within StrikeReach strikes it first (GuardResponded), else it hunts nearest civilian within HuntRadius and Strikes (CombatActionResolver) or steps one tile toward prey; mauled civilians survive at 1 HP (predators keep killing each other to self-cap). WitnessStep (Hourly, 45): scans the LAST HOUR of CombatResolved events (window derived from stamp, no early-break because multi-day catch-up isn't stamp-monotone), writes real ActorMemory (witnessed_attack / reported_attack) — the store's first runtime writer — and converges guards one tile toward the attacker. CompanionFollowStep (PerTick, 21): heel-follow + removes fallen companions loudly. CompanionGuardStep (Hourly, 42): each companion strikes one hostile within reach of player or self. FILES: Assets/Scripts/Simulation/Living/CascadeSystems.cs (PredationSystem + WitnessResponseSystem), CompanionSystem.cs
LESSONS: This is the world's first EVENT->EVENT cascade (attack -> witnessed+remembered -> watch converges+strikes) and the design charter for determinism: stateless instances seeded off the boundary stamp + both actor ids, so same world -> same bites. CADENCE-WRITER CONFLICT: predation, witness, and companion-guard all MoveTo/ApplyVitals the SAME actors, all Hourly, and all run AFTER the PerTick ScheduleStep already moved everyone — so an actor's final position/HP in one Advance is whichever hourly step touched it last. Predation deliberately does NOT kill civilians (only 1-HP maul) after lethal strikes depopulated whole towns over 58 travel-days.

## ARCHITECTURE SUMMARY
THE CONTRACT AS IT ACTUALLY EXISTS. The nominal sim→presentation boundary is IDomainSimulationAdapter (Assets/Scripts/Presentation/Ember/Adapters/IDomainSimulationAdapter.cs), an aggregate deliberately split into 6 role interfaces — IEmberSimulationClock (sink+source), IEmberHudReadModel, IWorldViewReadModel, IPlayerCommandSink, IConsultFateOracle, IEmberSaveBridge — so narrow consumers depend on a slice. One implementation, DomainSimulationAdapter (24 partials, wraps Domain WorldState, imports Domain freely; the presentation assembly never imports Domain THROUGH it because the adapter projects to flat DTOs — SpawnableActor, ActorViewState, *Row structs — that carry no Domain type). Resolution is a scene-scoped singleton, EmberDomainAdapterLocator.Current (Register overwrites silently). THE CANONICAL LOOP: EmberTickDriver (0.8333s/tick, 1440 ticks/game-day) → EmberWorldHost.OnTick → WorldViewProjector.ProjectTick(n) → adapter.AdvanceTick(n) {drain off-thread LLM applies, WorldTickComposer.Advance(world), PublishEventEchoes, PublishFieldMirror} → Project() {for each ActorView, TryReadActor(id/name)→ActorViewState→SetTarget} → EventLogHudPanel.Render. Between ticks the UI pumps ProjectWorldViewsNow() so hostile chase (1 cell/0.45s) reads at full speed. UI/HUD PULL every frame (InGameUiController.Update reads ICombatHudSource, spell slots, world events; screens read *Source DTOs). WHERE THE CONTRACT IS BYPASSED — five distinct escape hatches: (1) STATIC MIRROR CHANNELS. The clean per-tick read-model is shadowed by ~10 global statics the adapter writes and views poll directly, outside any interface: RuntimeFieldMirror (hour/minute/day/crop stage), RuntimeWeatherMirror, RuntimeCaravanMirror, RuntimeNpcDensity.Cap, WorldCombatFeedbackFeed (hit/felled/enemy-strike stamps), NpcEventEchoFeed (128-slot ring), RuntimeInteriorInfo, plus one-shot signals (ScreenRequestSignal, WorldEncounterSignal, WorldEncounterStingFeed). The stamp-not-consume pattern (a monotonic counter each view remembers) exists precisely because MANY views fan out from one event and a consume-flag would let one view eat it. These exist because the WorldSceneDirector and its builders run BEFORE the adapter's first tick, so there is no read-model to inject — the static channel is the only way to hand data forward. (2) DECORATIVE COORDINATES THE SIM NEVER PROVIDES. Building footprints DO come from sim truth (Simulation.WorldDirector.SettlementLayoutStrategy → BuildingPlacement.OriginX/Z), and terrain from real WorldGeography (WorldGeoSampler). But everything else in the realized scene is invented by WorldSceneDirector: plaza table/benches/well at hardcoded local offsets, region banner at (3.2,0,3.2) hued from RegionId, mine angle = seed%360, farm-belt angle = seed%360+137. Farm PLOT COUNT derives from HARDCODED tier populations (Capital 180k, City 75k, Town 6k…) NOT the sim's TotalPopulation. NPC billboard caps per settlement kind are a hardcoded table. Which 3 buildings become tavern/temple/shop is just "the first three in the layout list." (3) SCATTER + COSMETIC MOTION INVENTED WITH UnityEngine.Random. Worldgen seeds most NPCs on ONE settlement tile, so EmberGeneratedActorSpawner fans them onto concentric square rings by spawn index (pure presentation math), and ActorView.EnableWander/idle-float/walk-bob/hit-shake all use UnityEngine.Random — explicitly firewalled from Domain/save (docs/DETERMINISM.md). (4) HOUR-GUESSING vs SCHEDULE-TRUTH — a live tension in the codebase. ## ARCHITECTURE SUMMARY
GemRB is a from-scratch C++ reinterpreter of BioWare's Infinity Engine (BG1/2, IWD1/2, PST, and their EE variants). CRITICAL FRAMING: it is NOT a procedural-generation engine. There is no world/terrain/settlement generation — every area, building, tileset, creature, item, store, dialogue and worldmap is authored CONTENT shipped in the original games' binary formats. The one true "generator" in the codebase is the PST modron-maze (Game::maze_header/maze_entry). GemRB's job is therefore to be a deterministic, single-threaded, tick-driven INTERPRETER of that data plus a rules VM (D&D 2e/3e) on top of it. Read every "generation" system below as a "load + place + simulate" system.

LAYERING PHILOSOPHY (three de-facto tiers, not enforced by folders):
(1) ENGINE/platform — plugin loader, resource manager, importers, streams, video/audio drivers, save serialization. Everything pluggable is a `Plugin` registered by a numeric SClass_ID and produced by `PluginMgr`; assets are pulled through `ResourceManager`'s ordered `searchPath` of `ResourceSource`s (KEY/BIF, override dirs, save archive). This tier knows file formats, not game rules.
(2) SIM/core — the `Game` (party + loaded `Map`s + time), the `Scriptable` object graph (Actor/Door/Container/InfoPoint/Area/Global), the BCS script VM (GameScript: Triggers/Actions/Objects), the `EffectQueue` opcode system, pathfinding, combat, dialogue, economy. This is where D&D rules live and where determinism matters.
(3) PRESENTATION — `WindowManager`/`View` tree, controls, `GameControl` (the world viewport), `FogRenderer`, and the Python GUIScript layer that draws all menus/HUD by calling back into the sim. The sim NEVER calls up into presentation directly except through named GUIScript entry points (e.g. `RunFunction("GUIWORLD","DeathWindow")`), which is the main up-call seam.

HOW SYSTEMS COMMUNICATE: a single global god-object `core` (class `Interface`) is the service locator — everything reaches time, RNG, game state, gamedata, video, plugins through it. Objects that can be scripted, targeted, or have position derive from one base `Scriptable`, giving uniform trigger/action queues, local variables, and a `globalID` used as a stable weak handle (you store IDs, resolve via `Map::GetScriptableByGlobalID`, so deletion is safe). Game logic mutates state by (a) queuing `Action`s onto a Scriptable, (b) applying `Effect`s through the `EffectQueue`, or (c) setting variables in per-scope `ieVarsMap` dictionaries (GLOBAL == the Game scriptable's locals; LOCALS == an area/actor's; also KAPUTZ in PST). Scripts observe the world only through `TriggerEntry`s deposited on scriptables and cleared each cycle.

WHAT KEEPS IT CONSISTENT: one authoritative clock. `Interface::GameLoop` runs at a fixed AI cadence (`Time.ticksPerSec`, default 15 Hz) decoupled from render FPS — the loop calls `GameLoop()` only when `time-lastGameUpdate >= oneTick`, then draws windows every iteration. All durations are expressed in ticks and derived quantities (round=6s, turn=60s, day, hour) live in one `TimeStruct`. Determinism is preserved by: a fixed update order (Game → each Map → area script → actor scripts by priority queue → doors → containers → infopoints → projectiles → effects → fog), a shared `game->Ticks` snapshot passed to everything in a frame so all movement/attacks resolve against the same instant, and STAGGERED script execution (`TickScripting` gates each object to `Ticks%16==globalID%16`) so the 15 Hz budget spreads AI across frames without changing outcomes. Two representations of every 2D quantity are kept rigorously separated by distinct C++ types so they can't be accidentally mixed: NavmapPoint (per-pixel world space) vs SearchmapPoint (pathfinding tiles, 16×12 px) vs FogPoint (visibility cells, 32 px). That type discipline is the single biggest structural safeguard in the rendering/data bridge.

FRAGILITY: the god-object `core` and the pervasive `HasFeature(GFFlags::...)` per-game conditionals (dozens of them, often with archaeological comments naming the exact area/creature that forced the branch) make behavior game-specific and hard to reason about globally; correctness is anchored by matching original-engine quirks rather than clean invariants.

## Simulation Cadence & Tick Order [sim]
DATA: `GlobalTimer` (core/GlobalTimer.cpp) holds viewport pan `goal`/`speed`, `shakeCounter`, and fade counters. `Game` holds `std::atomic_uint32_t GameTime`, `RealTime`, `CombatCounter`, and time-override counters. `Scriptable` carries per-object `Ticks`, `AdjustedTicks` (haste/slow), `ScriptTicks`, `IdleTicks`, `TriggerCountdown`. FLOW: Per AI tick: `Game::UpdateScripts()` → `Update()` (own global script) → for each loaded Map `Map::UpdateScripts()`: MarkNewFrame on traversability cache → `GenerateQueues`+`SortQueues` (bucket actors by priority/visibility) → run AREA script → snapshot `time=game->Ticks` → iterate RunScripts queue back-to-front: `actor->fxqueue.Cleanup()`, skip if helpless/timestopped, `actor->Update()` (runs BCS + processes action queue), `UpdateActorState`, `SetSpeed`, then `DoStepForActor` (movement with bump-avoidance repath) → Display queue fx cleanup → door `Update()`s → container `Update()`s → infopoint/trap `Update()`s → `UpdateEffects`/`UpdateProjectiles`/`UpdateFog`. Back in Game: manage `CombatCounter` (150-tick decay to switch battle song), decrement override timers, unload maps beyond `MAX_MAPS_LOADED`, check death/party-overflow. `GlobalTimer::Update` separately advances viewport/fade and increments `RealTime`; it FREEZES script advance while fading/shaking or in freeze-script dialog.
FILES: core/GlobalTimer.cpp, core/Game.cpp (UpdateScripts, AdvanceTime), core/Map.cpp (UpdateScripts, GenerateQueues), core/Scriptable/Scriptable.cpp (TickScripting)
LESSONS: ROBUST: single fixed order + one shared `game->Ticks` per frame = deterministic, reproducible sim (needed for save compatibility and multiplayer aspirations). ROBUST: `TickScripting` staggering (`Ticks%16==globalID%16`, forced for on-screen/triggered/idle-15 actors) amortizes AI cost without altering results. ROBUST: running an actor's whole script at once (comment at Map.cpp:763) deliberately avoids a race where a mid-script cutscene→dialog transition could be exploited. FRAGILE: the ordering has accreted special-cases (fuzzie/Avenger/wjp comments) tied to specific original-game bugs.

## BCS Script Virtual Machine (GameScript) [sim]
DATA: Compiled `.bcs`/`.bs` scripts parse into `Script`→`std::vector<ResponseBlock*>`; each block = a `Condition` (list of `Trigger`s) + a `ResponseSet` (weighted `Response`s, each a list of `Action`s). Triggers are C++ functors in Triggers.cpp keyed by IDS name; Actions in Actions.cpp; target resolution in Objects.cpp/Targets.cpp/Matching.cpp. A `Scriptable` owns `std::array<GameScript*,MAX_SCRIPTS=8>` (OVERRIDE/AREA/SPECIFICS/CLASS/RACE/GENERAL/DEFAULT slots), an `actionQueue` (std::list<Action*>) + `CurrentAction`, and observed `std::list<TriggerEntry>` (id+params, e.g. trigger_attackedby, trigger_entered, trigger_died) plus `StoredObjects` (LastAttacker/LastSeen/LastTarget…).
FLOW: Split into DECISION and EXECUTION (GameScript.cpp header comment). Decision: `Scriptable::ExecuteScript` (from TickScripting) loops script slots 0..N; `GameScript::Update` (GameScript.cpp:1874) evaluates each block's condition against accumulated triggers; first passing block whose queue is clear (unless Continue()d) clears the action queue and enqueues its response's actions; `Continue()` chains blocks. Execution: `Scriptable::ProcessActions` pops the queue and runs `CurrentAction` across ticks — blocking actions (WalkTo, Attack) persist until complete; instant ones run and pop. Triggers are cleared after a block changes state (`ClearTriggers`).
FILES: core/GameScript/GameScript.cpp (Update), core/GameScript/Actions.cpp, Triggers.cpp, Objects.cpp, Targets.cpp, Matching.cpp, ParseBCS.cpp; core/Scriptable/Scriptable.cpp (ExecuteScript, ProcessActions)
LESSONS: ROBUST: strict condition→queue→execute separation means perception (triggers) can't tear the world mid-action; blocking actions naturally span ticks via `CurrentAction`. ROBUST: 8 prioritized script slots give layered AI (override buffs/charm beat class beat default movement). ROBUST: interruptibility flags (IF_NOINT, CurrentActionInterruptible, ACF_OVERRIDE) protect cutscenes/dialog from being clobbered. FRAGILE: `lastResponseBlock` re-entry heuristics carry per-game hacks (GF_SKIPUPDATE_HACK history) documented with specific area IDs.

## Pathfinding & Movement (Theta*) [sim]
DATA: Two overlaid grids with distinct types: `NavmapPoint`(=Point, per-pixel) for movement, `SearchmapPoint` (tiles, navmap/16×12) for path search. `Path`=`std::vector<PathNode>{point,orient,waypoint}` + currentStep. `PathMapFlags` bitfield per searchmap cell (PASSABLE/TRAVEL/NO_SEE/SIDEWALL/DOOR_*/PC/NPC). `TraversabilityCache` caches per-frame blocking (actors as dynamic blockers). LESSONS: ROBUST: separating search resolution (coarse tiles) from motion resolution (fine pixels) with SEPARATE C++ TYPES makes any-angle movement possible AND makes accidental unit-mixing a compile error — the standout structural safeguard. ROBUST: Theta* + P-controller gives smooth movement without storing dense per-pixel paths. ROBUST: per-frame `MarkNewFrame` traversability cache keeps dynamic actor-blocking correct without recomputing the static map.

## Save / Load Serialization [engine]
DATA: A save is a directory/archive: `GAM` (party, journals, globals/KAPUTZ, world time, reputation, saved+plane positions, familiars, maze), one `ARE` per visited/dirty area (dynamic layer), `SAV` archive bundling area+CRE+STO blobs, `WMP` worldmap state, plus screenshot/portraits. `SaveGame`/`SaveGameIterator` (core/SaveGameIterator.h) enumerate slots; `SaveGameAREExtractor` (core/SaveGameAREExtractor.h) lazily pulls a specific ARE out of the running save archive on demand (`extractARE`, `isRunningSaveGame`, `updateSaveGame(offset)`).
FLOW: Save: `CreateSaveGame` serializes Game→GAM (GAMImporter), each loaded/modified Map→ARE (AREImporter write path) into a SAV (SAVImporter). Load: read GAM to reconstruct `Game` (party CREs, time, vars), then areas are demand-loaded from the save archive via the AREExtractor when first visited, overlaying the base ARE. Because areas are lazily extracted, a running game keeps its save archive as a live `ResourceSource` in the search path.
FILES: core/SaveGameIterator.h/.cpp, core/SaveGame.h, core/SaveGameAREExtractor.cpp, plugins/GAMImporter, plugins/SAVImporter, plugins/AREImporter, plugins/CREImporter
LESSONS: ROBUST: serialize only the dynamic (ARE/CRE/STO) delta over immutable base assets — saves stay small and forward-compatible; the AREExtractor's lazy per-area extraction avoids rehydrating the whole world at load. ROBUST: reusing the SAME importers for read and write means load/save share format code. FRAGILE: many `Actor`/`Store` structs carry `unknown[]`/IgnoredFields explicitly preserved byte-for-byte purely to round-trip original saves — real complexity hides in save compatibility.

## Data→Render Bridge: Coordinate Systems & Packed Channels [presentation]
DATA: THREE coordinate spaces as distinct C++ types (compile-time separation): NavmapPoint(=Point) world PIXELS; `SearchmapPoint` pathfinding TILES = navmap/(16,12) (`ToNavmapPoint()`=x*16,y*12); `FogPoint` visibility CELLS = navmap/32 (`FogRenderer::CELL_SIZE`). The area's data channels are packed into ONE uint32 per searchmap tile in `TileProps` (core/Map.h:256): SEARCH_MAP=0xff000000 (passability/PathMapFlags), MATERIAL=0x00ff0000 (terrain sound/type), ELEVATION=0x0000ff00 (heightmap, 128=sea level), LIGHTING=0x000000ff (palette index into lightmap). `Bitmap ExploredBitmap`/`VisibleBitmap` store fog-of-war at FogPoint resolution.
FLOW: `Map::DrawMap()` (Map.cpp:1172) documents a fixed 9-step strategy: 1 draw background(MOS/animated) → 2 weather overlays + target reticles → 3 build two wall stencils (COVERANIMS + opaque) → 4 set anim-wall stencil → 5 draw background animations (STENCIL_GREEN) → 6 set wall stencil → 7 draw scriptables sorted by effective height (occluded by walls via stencil) → 8 draw fog (BLENDED) → 9 draw overhead text. `UpdateFog` recomputes VisibleBitmap from each explorer actor's LOS each tick; `FogRenderer::DrawFog` converts FogPoint→screen. Queries (`GetBlocked`, `GetHeight`, `GetLighting`, `QueryMaterial`) read the packed channels via typed SearchmapPoint accessors.
FILES: core/Region.h (Point/SearchmapPoint/FogPoint types), core/Map.h (TileProps), core/Map.cpp (DrawMap, UpdateFog, GetBlocked/GetHeight/GetLighting), core/FogRenderer.h/.cpp, core/PathFinder.cpp
LESSONS: ROBUST (the crown jewel): four semantically different rasters (collision, sound-material, elevation, light) are stored in one cache-friendly uint32 grid yet accessed through named, masked, TYPED getters — dense storage without losing type safety. ROBUST: separate Nav/Search/Fog point TYPES make it a COMPILE ERROR to feed pixel coords to the pathfinder or fog to collision — the structural discipline that keeps data and render honest. ROBUST: stencil-based wall occlusion lets 2D actors correctly hide behind pre-rendered geometry using per-tile height, bridging authored art and dynamic sprites.

## ARCHITECTURE SUMMARY
Dwarf Fortress 0.47.04 (the final "legacy" ASCII release, Feb 2020) is a single monolithic closed-source C++ binary (C:/Users/msbel/Downloads/dwarf-fortress-legacy/Dwarf Fortress.exe, ~17 MB) wrapped around FMOD (audio) and SDL/OpenGL (via bundled msvcp/msvcr100 runtimes). ALL simulation, worldgen, AI, pathfinding, combat physics, and rendering live INSIDE that binary and are not source-visible. What the repo exposes — and what this atlas therefore inventories — is the game's DATA CONTRACT: a schema-driven declarative layer (raw/) that the engine parses at world-genesis, plus a config/presentation layer (data/) it reads at boot and per-frame. The layering philosophy is a strict three-tier separation of concerns enforced by WHEN each tier is read. (1) The RAW/DEFINITION tier (raw/objects/*.txt) is a homogeneous bracket-token grammar — every file is a flat stream of [TOKEN:ARG:ARG...] lines opened by [OBJECT:CREATURE|ENTITY|MATERIAL|BODY|ITEM_WEAPON|REACTION|INTERACTION|BUILDING|LANGUAGE|...]. This is the "source code" of the world's ontology: it defines TYPES, not instances. It is read once, at Create-New-World, and BAKED into the region save; the readme warns that removing raw entries corrupts existing saves and that additions only take effect in newly generated worlds — i.e. the raws are a compile-time schema frozen into each world's binary snapshot. (2) The CONFIG tier (data/init/*.txt) is read at every boot and tunes the running engine — display mode, FPS caps, keybindings, worldgen parameter presets, gameplay toggles (temperature/weather/economy/invaders/caveins), and the announcement routing table. (3) The PRESENTATION tier (data/art/*.bmp tilesets, data/init/colors.txt, optional raw/graphics/*) maps abstract simulation cells to glyphs/colors. Systems do NOT call each other directly through code the modder can see; instead they COMMUNICATE THROUGH A SHARED SYMBOL NAMESPACE. A creature references a body plan by token (BODY:HUMANOID_NECK), a body plan references tissues by token (VERTEBRATE_TISSUE_LAYERS:SKIN:FAT:MUSCLE:BONE), tissues reference materials by token (TISSUE_MATERIAL:LOCAL_CREATURE_MAT:SKIN), an entity references creatures/items/positions by token (CREATURE:DWARF, WEAPON:ITEM_WEAPON_PICK, POSITION:SHERIFF), a reaction references buildings/reagents/products by token (BUILDING:SMELTER, REAGENT:...:METAL_ORE:COPPER), and an interaction references syndromes/creature-flags by token. Consistency is kept by three structural mechanisms: TEMPLATES (material_template_default, tissue_template_default — shared physical-property prototypes instantiated with USE_MATERIAL_TEMPLATE and locally overridden), BODY_DETAIL_PLANs and CREATURE_VARIATIONs (parameterized macros with ARG1..ARGn substitution that stamp hundreds of tokens onto a creature from one line, e.g. APPLY_CREATURE_VARIATION:STANDARD_BIPED_GAITS:900:711:...), and a late-binding SELECT/SET grammar (SELECT_CASTE, SET_BP_GROUP, SELECT_TISSUE_LAYER, PLUS_TISSUE_LAYER) that re-opens already-declared structures to layer modifiers on top. The unifying data-model philosophy is EVERYTHING-IS-A-MATERIAL: rock, blood, magma, wheat-beer, steel, and a demon's divine metal are all the same MATERIAL struct with the same ~40 physical fields (SOLID_DENSITY, IMPACT_YIELD/FRACTURE, SHEAR_YIELD, MAX_EDGE, MELTING_POINT, SPEC_HEAT), so ONE combat-physics solver and ONE temperature solver in the binary handle every substance uniformly — the raws only supply coefficients. This is what makes emergent play robust: the engine has few special cases; novelty comes from new coefficient rows, not new code paths. The fragility is the flip side: the schema is positional and untyped (ATTACK:EDGE:20000:4000:slash:slashes:NO_SUB:1250 — meaning is by ordinal position, a misplaced colon silently changes semantics), there is no referential-integrity checker at author time (a dangling token token surfaces only as a worldgen crash — the release notes are full of "fixed worldgen crash involving..."), and the frozen-into-save contract means schema evolution is a minefield. The data→render boundary is a channel model: the simulation is a 3D voxel grid (embark tiles x, y, and Z-levels — worldgen declares LEVELS_ABOVE_GROUND:15, CAVERN_LAYER_COUNT:3, HAVE_BOTTOM_LAYER — the underworld), each cell carrying material+state+temperature+contaminant channels, and the renderer collapses one Z-slice to an 80x25 glyph grid where tile index + 3-color (fg:bg:bright) triples come from creature/material DISPLAY tokens, d_init tile-assignment tokens (TRACK_*, TREE_*, WOUND_COLOR_*), and the loaded tileset BMP.

## World / Terrain Generation [engine (driver) + data (parameters)]
DATA: Driven by data/init/world_gen.txt: a list of named [WORLD_GEN] presets (TITLE:LARGE ISLAND) each a flat parameter block. Core fields: [DIM:257:257] world grid size; six terrain scalar fields each given as min:max:count:count buckets — [ELEVATION:1:400:1600:1600], [RAINFALL], [TEMPERATURE], [DRAINAGE], [VOLCANISM], [SAVAGERY] — plus per-octave *_FREQUENCY:1:1:1:1:1:1 mesh-frequency weights (fractal/diamond-square noise controls). Hydrology/erosion: [EROSION_CYCLE_COUNT:250], [RIVER_MINS], [OROGRAPHIC_PRECIPITATION:1], [PERIODICALLY_ERODE_EXTREMES]. Biome quotas: [REGION_COUNTS:FOREST:1008:3:2], MOUNTAINS/OCEAN/GLACIER/etc. Z-axis/underground: [CAVERN_LAYER_COUNT:3], [LEVELS_ABOVE_GROUND:15], [HAVE_BOTTOM_LAYER_1/2] (the underworld), CAVE min/max sizes. History sim: [END_YEAR:1050], [BEAST_END_YEAR], population caps ([TOTAL_CIV_NUMBER:40], [TOTAL_CIV_POPULATION:15000], [SITE_CAP:1500]), and a whole roster of mythic-threat seeds (MEGABEAST_CAP, TITAN_NUMBER, DEMON_NUMBER, VAMPIRE_NUMBER, WEREBEAST_NUMBER, SECRET_NUMBER, EVIL_CLOUD_NUMBER) with attack-trigger thresholds (WEREBEAST_ATTACK_TRIGGER:50:5000:50000 = pop:created-wealth:exported-wealth). Good/evil region distribution via GOOD_SQ_COUNTS/EVIL_SQ_COUNTS.
FLOW: Runs ONCE per world at genesis, not per tick. Pipeline (inferred, all inside the binary): (1) generate elevation/rain/temp/drainage/volcanism/savagery noise meshes at the frequency weights; (2) run EROSION_CYCLE_COUNT hydraulic passes carving rivers to RIVER_MINS; (3) place biomes to REGION_COUNTS quotas; (4) carve CAVERN_LAYER_COUNT underground layers and the underworld; (5) seed civilizations and mythic threats; (6) fast-forward HISTORY from year 0 to END_YEAR, simulating civ expansion, wars, villainy, religion spread, megabeast rampages as discrete historical events, writing an XML legends log. Can be launched headless from CLI: 'Dwarf Fortress.exe' -gen <id> <seed> <title> exports region files + a picture then quits. Output is a region save folder (data/save/region*, absent in this fresh copy) that freezes the world; fortress/adventure play then loads and continues it at real tick cadence.
FILES: data/init/world_gen.txt; command line.txt (headless -gen); data/save/ (output, empty here)
LESSONS: ROBUST: parameters are pure data, so an infinite space of worlds comes from one deterministic algorithm keyed by (seed,params) — reproducible, shareable as a tiny text preset. The min:max:weight bucket idiom lets one field express both range and distribution shape. Separating BEAST_END_YEAR from END_YEAR lets the mythic age close before the historical age, shaping narrative. FRAGILE: worldgen is the single most crash-prone subsystem — release notes repeatedly cite 'worldgen crash involving civs attacking ruins', 'criminals and arrest', 'busted param newlines'; because history is simulated to completion before play, a rare interaction bug aborts the entire multi-minute gen. Parameters are positional and unvalidated; a stray space historically broke param parsing.

## Settlement / Site & Building Generation [engine (placement) + data (constraints & workshop schema)]
DATA: Two sub-models. (a) SITE placement is constrained per-civilization in raw/objects/entity_default.txt: [DEFAULT_SITE_TYPE:CAVE_DETAILED], [LIKES_SITE]/[TOLERATES_SITE:CITY], [START_BIOME]/[SETTLEMENT_BIOME:MOUNTAIN]/[EXCLUSIVE_START_BIOME], [BIOME_SUPPORT:MOUNTAIN:3] (carrying-capacity weights), [MAX_SITE_POP_NUMBER:120], [MAX_POP_NUMBER:10000]. (b) In-fortress BUILDINGS are defined in raw/objects/building_custom.txt as [BUILDING_WORKSHOP:SOAP_MAKER] / [BUILDING_FURNACE:...] with a footprint grid: [DIM:3:3], [WORK_LOCATION:2:2], per-cell [TILE:x:y:passable-char-triples] and [COLOR:...] ASCII art, [BLOCK:...] passability mask, [BUILD_LABOR:SOAP_MAKER], [BUILD_KEY:CUSTOM_SHIFT_S] hotkey, and [BUILD_ITEM:1:BUCKET:...] material/quantity requirements with modifier flags (EMPTY, BUILDMAT, WORTHLESS_STONE_ONLY, CAN_USE_ARTIFACT). Standard buildings (smelter, forge, still) are hardcoded in the binary; only custom workshops are raw-defined.
FLOW: Site generation runs during worldgen and off-screen world simulation (release notes: mercenaries buy houses, richer figures 'build up their own city towers'). Player-fortress buildings are placed interactively (designation -> job) then CONSTRUCTED by a dwarf executing a build job that consumes the BUILD_ITEM reagents; once built, the workshop becomes a job-issuing station ticked by the job system. Building footprint/passability feeds the pathfinding grid immediately on completion.
FILES: raw/objects/entity_default.txt (site constraints); raw/objects/building_custom.txt; data/help/menu_buildjob*, menu_make_buildings
LESSONS: ROBUST: the same ASCII-art tile grid that DEFINES a workshop is also what RENDERS it — one authoring artifact serves both simulation footprint and display, eliminating a data-sync class of bug. Biome-support weights let civ density emerge from terrain rather than being hand-placed. FRAGILE: workshop tile grids are hand-encoded coordinate-by-coordinate (TILE:2:2:...:COLOR:2:2:...) with no visual authoring, extremely error-prone; standard buildings being binary-locked means modders can only add, never reshape core industry.

## Simulation Game Loop & Temporal Cadences [engine (opaque core loop)]
DATA: The tick scheduler lives entirely in the binary; the raws expose its CADENCE KNOBS as durations. Time is measured in ticks; many actions declare their own periods: interactions have [CDI:WAIT_PERIOD:20/30/100] and [IE_TIME_RANGE:200:300]; attacks have [ATTACK_PREPARE_AND_RECOVER:3:3] (windup:recovery ticks) so combat is a state machine over ticks, not instantaneous; gaits encode speed as tick-cost per step (GAIT full-speed:build-up-time:...); syndromes schedule effects on START/PEAK/END tick offsets ([CE_...:START:0], CE_FLASH_TILE:FREQUENCY:2000:1000). Frame cadence is config: data/init/init.txt [FPS_CAP:100] (simulation ticks/sec ceiling) vs [G_FPS_CAP:50] (graphics redraw/sec) — a DECOUPLED sim-rate/render-rate design; [PARTIAL_PRINT], [VSYNC:OFF]. Calendar granularity (BODY_SIZE:year:day:size, seasonal plant [SPRING][SUMMER][AUTUMN][WINTER], entity [ACTIVE_SEASON:AUTUMN]) shows a nested year>season>day>tick hierarchy.
FLOW: Inferred main loop: input poll -> advance N sim ticks (throttled to FPS_CAP, 0 when paused) -> render one Z-slice at G_FPS_CAP. Within a sim tick the engine walks active units/items/liquids/temperature/jobs. Cadences are staggered: fast (combat prepare/recover, fluid flow, temperature diffusion) every tick; medium (creature needs, job assignment, pathing) on coarser multi-tick periods; slow (seasons, migrations, caravans on entity ACTIVE_SEASON, aging on BODY_SIZE year marks). Off-map world simulation continues on a much coarser historical cadence. Pause ([PAUSE_ON_LOAD], BOX-flagged announcements force-pause) halts the sim tick but not the render/input loop.
FILES: data/init/init.txt (FPS_CAP/G_FPS_CAP/VSYNC); cadence knobs scattered across all raw/objects (CDI WAIT_PERIOD, ATTACK_PREPARE_AND_RECOVER, GAIT, syndrome START/PEAK/END)
LESSONS: ROBUST: decoupling FPS_CAP (sim) from G_FPS_CAP (render) lets a heavy fortress keep simulating while the display coasts — the classic remedy for 'FPS death' is fewer sim entities, not a render change. Per-action tick periods (WAIT_PERIOD, PREPARE_AND_RECOVER) push scheduling INTO the data, so the core loop stays a uniform iterator. FRAGILE: single-threaded tick over an ever-growing unit/item population means late-fortress performance degrades roughly linearly; there is no data-visible spatial partitioning knob, so the modder cannot tune the loop's scaling.

## Agent AI — Needs, Personality & Values [sim (modeled from creature+entity raws)]
DATA: Per-creature in raw/objects/creature_standard.txt: attributes as percentile distributions [PHYS_ATT_RANGE:STRENGTH:450:950:1150:1250:1350:1550:2250] (7 bins, 1000=human median) and mental [MENT_ATT_RANGE:FOCUS:...]; innate [PERSONALITY:IMMODERATION:0:55:100] / STRESS_VULNERABILITY / GREED (min:median:max Big-Five-like facets); appetites/dependencies [ALCOHOL_DEPENDENT], [CAVE_ADAPT], [SMELL_TRIGGER:90], sensory [LOW_LIGHT_VISION], thermal [HOMEOTHERM:10067]; social flavor as MANNERISM_* tokens (twenty+ idle-behavior tics) and [PREFSTRING:beards]. CULTURAL needs come from the owning entity's [VALUE:CRAFTSMANSHIP:50], [VALUE:NATURE:-15] etc. (33 values on a -50..+50 scale) which set what satisfies/distresses a member. Cognitive gates: [INTELLIGENT], [CAN_LEARN], [CAN_SPEAK], [TRANCES], [STRANGE_MOODS] (the artifact-creation fugue).
FLOW: Each conscious unit is ticked by a need/mood evaluator (in binary) that compares current state against personality thresholds and entity values, producing 'thoughts' and a stress level; unmet needs (drink for ALCOHOL_DEPENDENT dwarves, worship, craftsmanship) accumulate negative thoughts driving the happiness->tantrum spiral. STRANGE_MOODS periodically seize an eligible dwarf to make an artifact. Attributes are rolled per-individual from the RANGE bins at creation, then rust/improve with use unless a syndrome sets NO_PHYS_ATT_GAIN/RUST.
FILES: raw/objects/creature_standard.txt (attributes/personality/mannerisms); raw/objects/entity_default.txt (VALUE/ETHIC); data/help/menu_professions, unit_profile
LESSONS: ROBUST: personality/attributes as DISTRIBUTIONS not fixed values means every individual dwarf is statistically unique from one shared template — deep character variety at near-zero authoring cost. Splitting innate personality (creature) from cultural values (entity) lets the same DWARF creature feel different inside a different civ. FRAGILE: the happiness model is notoriously tuning-sensitive (release notes: 'marriages dissolved frequently', 'friendly visiting breaking furniture') — emergent psychology means small coefficient changes cascade into fort-wide tantrum spirals that are hard to predict from the data alone.

## Agent AI — Jobs, Labors & Schedules [sim (job graph over entity+reaction+building tokens)]
DATA: The labor taxonomy is declared per-civ as [PERMITTED_JOB:MINER|CARPENTER|MASON|WEAPONSMITH|...] (dozens) in entity_default.txt, and per-creature [PROFESSION_NAME:CRAFTSMAN:craftsdwarf:craftsdwarves] gives the display name. Priority/repeat is managed via the manager/work-orders UI. Idle dwarves pull from a global job queue each scheduling pass.
FILES: raw/objects/entity_default.txt (PERMITTED_JOB); raw/objects/reaction_*.txt; raw/objects/building_custom.txt; data/init/d_init.txt (PATH_COST/STORE_DIST); data/help/menu_manager, menu_orders, menu_jobunitlist
LESSONS: ROBUST: jobs are a data-defined bipartite matching (labor-tokens <-> dwarf-capabilities) so adding an industry is adding raws, not code; skill-through-use ties directly to the same SKILL token used by combat, unifying progression. STORE_DIST_* knobs externalize the hauling heuristics that dominate late-game FPS. FRAGILE: reagent reservation + pathing over a huge item pool is the real FPS sink; the matching is greedy per-pass with no data-visible planner, so pathological hauling (dwarves crossing the map for a single sock) emerges and can only be mitigated with stockpile layout, not tuning.

## Agent AI — Combat & Anatomy Resolution [engine (physics solver) + sim (body/attack/material data)]
DATA: Combat is a materials-physics simulation, not hit-points. Inputs: WEAPON attacks [ATTACK:EDGE:20000:4000:slash:slashes:NO_SUB:1250] = (type, contact-area, penetration/velocity-mult, verb, verb3p, sub-part, prepare-recover-implied); weapon [SIZE], [TWO_HANDED], [MINIMUM_SIZE], [MATERIAL_SIZE], [SHOOT_FORCE]/[SHOOT_MAXVEL] for ranged. Natural attacks on the creature (ATTACK:BITE:CHILD_BODYPART_GROUP..., ATTACK:SCRATCH...NAIL, with ATTACK_SKILL, ATTACK_CONTACT_PERC, ATTACK_PENETRATION_PERC, ATTACK_FLAG_EDGE/CANLATCH, ATTACK_PRIORITY). The struck body is a graph of [BP:RUA:right upper arm][CON:UB][LIMB][CATEGORY:ARM_UPPER] parts (raw/objects/body_default.txt) each wrapped in ordered TISSUE_LAYERs (skin/fat/muscle/bone/cartilage at thickness ratios from BODY_DETAIL_PLAN:VERTEBRATE_TISSUE_LAYERS). Both weapon material and each tissue's material carry the SAME physical fields — IMPACT/SHEAR/TENSILE _YIELD/_FRACTURE, STRAIN_AT_YIELD, MAX_EDGE, SOLID_DENSITY (material_template_default.txt, tissue_template_default.txt). Tissues add HEALING_RATE, VASCULAR, PAIN_RECEPTORS, TL_MAJOR_ARTERIES, HAS_NERVES.
FLOW: Per attack tick: engine computes momentum = f(attacker STRENGTH, weapon SIZE/velocity, gait), then for each tissue layer in order solves whether contact stress exceeds that material's SHEAR (cutting) or IMPACT (blunt) YIELD/FRACTURE given edge sharpness (MAX_EDGE) and contact area — cutting through skin->fat->muscle->bone until momentum is spent, producing severing, denting, bruising, arterial bleeding (from TL_MAJOR_ARTERIES/VASCULAR), pain (PAIN_RECEPTORS -> stun/KO), or fracture. Wrestling is a separate grab/lock/choke state machine. Every micro-outcome is routed to the announcement channel (data/init/announcements.txt has ~200 COMBAT_* event types: COMBAT_PARRY, COMBAT_WRESTLE_CHOKEHOLD, COMBAT_GRAB_TEAR).
FILES: raw/objects/item_weapon.txt; raw/objects/body_default.txt; raw/objects/b_detail_plan_default.txt; raw/objects/tissue_template_default.txt; raw/objects/material_template_default.txt; raw/objects/item_armor.txt/item_shield.txt; data/init/announcements.txt (COMBAT_*); data/init/d_init.txt (WOUND_COLOR_*)
LESSONS: ROBUST: ONE stress solver over a UNIVERSAL material struct means a steel sword vs a bronze breastplate, a bite through fat, and a boulder crushing a skull all use the same math — armor 'works' emergently because it is just another high-yield tissue layer in the stack. Layered anatomy makes wounds locational and consequential (severed nerve, lost function) for free. FRAGILE: the physics is exquisitely coefficient-dependent — a wrong YIELD row makes a material absurdly strong/weak; the release notes cite teleport-through-walls and sparring bugs, showing the state machine's edge cases; and the sheer per-layer computation makes large battles a performance cliff.

## Creature & Caste Definition System [data (schema) -> sim]
DATA: raw/objects/creature_*.txt (24 files, creature_standard.txt alone 231 KB). [CREATURE:DWARF] holds creature-level tokens then splits into [CASTE:MALE]/[CASTE:FEMALE] (or arbitrary castes) via [SELECT_CASTE:ALL]/[SELECT_CASTE:MALE]. A caste-level token placed before any CASTE is auto-applied to all later castes. Composition tokens pull in shared structure: [BODY:...], [BODY_DETAIL_PLAN:...], [USE_MATERIAL_TEMPLATE:BLOOD:BLOOD_TEMPLATE], [USE_TISSUE_TEMPLATE], [APPLY_CREATURE_VARIATION:STANDARD_BIPED_GAITS:...]. Appearance is procedural: [BODY_APPEARANCE_MODIFIER:HEIGHT:75:95:98:100:102:105:125] and per-part [SET_BP_GROUP:BY_CATEGORY:EYE][BP_APPEARANCE_MODIFIER:CLOSE_SET:...] with APP_MOD_NOUN/DESC_RANGE for text descriptions. Life cycle [BODY_SIZE:0:0:3000]/[BODY_SIZE:12:0:60000], [BABY:1]/[CHILD:12], [MAXAGE:150:170]. Flags gate behavior: INTELLIGENT, CAN_LEARN, CANOPENDOORS, BENIGN, EQUIPS, [CREATURE_CLASS:MAMMAL/GENERAL_POISON] (used by syndromes/positions).
FLOW: Parsed at worldgen into a creature-type registry frozen in the save. Individuals are instantiated by rolling caste, then each *_RANGE/*_APPEARANCE_MODIFIER distribution, then applying variations/templates, producing a unique unit. CREATURE_VARIATION and BODY_DETAIL_PLAN are expanded at parse time (macro substitution of ARG1..ARGn), so the stored type is fully flattened.
FILES: raw/objects/creature_standard.txt + 23 other creature_*.txt; raw/objects/c_variation_default.txt; raw/objects/descriptor_color/pattern/shape_*.txt
LESSONS: ROBUST: the SELECT/SET late-binding grammar plus templates/variations gives single-source-of-truth reuse (fix a gait once, all bipeds inherit) with local override — classic prototype inheritance in a text DSL. Caste-before-declaration auto-application removes boilerplate. Everything (blood, sweat, nails, gait, secretions) is opt-in via tokens, so a creature is exactly as complex as authored. FRAGILE: the same flattening that gives reuse makes provenance opaque (a dwarf's final tissue stack comes from 4 detail plans + templates + manual layers interleaved with SELECT statements — hard to read); and because types bake into saves, editing a creature's body destabilizes existing worlds (readme explicitly warns).

## Entity / Civilization & Culture System [data (schema) -> sim]
DATA: raw/objects/entity_default.txt (55 KB) — [ENTITY:MOUNTAIN] is the dwarven civ. Binds a playable race [CREATURE:DWARF]+[TRANSLATION:DWARF]; a materiel whitelist ([DIGGER], [WEAPON:...], [ARMOR:...:COMMON/UNCOMMON], HELM/GLOVES/SHOES/PANTS/SHIELD/SIEGEAMMO/TRAPCOMP/TOY/[TOOL:...], [CLOTHING], [SUBTERRANEAN_CLOTHING]); an economy ([CURRENCY:COPPER:1/SILVER:5/GOLD:15], [CURRENCY_BY_YEAR], preferences METAL_PREF/GEM_PREF/STONE_PREF, resource use flags USE_CAVE_ANIMALS/INDOOR_FARMING/OUTDOOR_GARDENS); a MORAL CODE of 23 [ETHIC:KILL_ENTITY_MEMBER:PUNISH_CAPITAL] entries on an ordinal severity scale (ACCEPTABLE<PERSONAL_MATTER<JUSTIFIED<...<SHUN<APPALLING<UNTHINKABLE, with PUNISH_* variants) and 33 [VALUE:LAW:30] cultural weights; site/biome placement rules; population caps; religion [RELIGION:PANTHEON]+[RELIGION_SPHERE:MOUNTAINS/METALS/...]; symbol-generation pools [SELECT_SYMBOL:WAR:NAME_WAR]/[CULL_SYMBOL:ALL:DOMESTIC]; and a POSITION hierarchy (see Crime/Law).
FLOW: Instantiated per-civ during worldgen; the ETHIC/VALUE vectors drive off-screen historical behavior (who goes to war, who trades, religious persecution — release notes detail world-gen villainy, alliances, guild/mercenary/temple founding all keyed on entity data). In-fort it governs the item menus offered, noble appointments, mandate generation, diplomacy stance, and what visitors/migrants arrive.
FILES: raw/objects/entity_default.txt; raw/objects/language_SYM.txt (symbol word pools); data/help/menu_nobles, menu_civlist, trading
LESSONS: ROBUST: encoding culture as two ordinal vectors (ETHICS severity + VALUES weight) makes inter-civ friction EMERGENT and comparable — the engine can numerically decide if an act offends a given civ, so diplomacy/crime/reputation all read the same tables. The materiel whitelist cleanly scopes a race's tech without new code. FRAGILE: the ethics system feeds so many subsystems (war, justice, thoughts, diplomacy) that mis-set values ripple widely; symbol/name pools use an opaque SELECT/SUBSELECT/CULL grammar that is hard to predict.

## Interaction / Syndrome / Magic System [data (schema) -> engine (effect executor)]
DATA: raw/objects/interaction_standard.txt + raw/interaction examples/*.txt (vampire, werebeast, secret/necromancy, region, disturbance, underground_special, shrine, bogeyman). An [INTERACTION:X] is a rule: [I_SOURCE:CREATURE_ACTION|SECRET|REGION|DISTURBANCE] fires it; [I_TARGET:A:CREATURE/CORPSE/LOCATION] with predicates [IT_LOCATION:CONTEXT_CREATURE], [IT_REQUIRES:MORTAL/CAN_LEARN], [IT_FORBIDDEN:NOT_LIVING], [IT_AFFECTED_CLASS:GENERAL_POISON], ranges; and [I_EFFECT:ADD_SYNDROME|ANIMATE|SUMMON_UNIT|CLEAN|MATERIAL_EMISSION] with [IE_TARGET], [IE_IMMEDIATE]. The payload is a [SYNDROME] block of timed CE_ (creature-effect) tokens: CE_PHYS_ATT_CHANGE, CE_SPEED_CHANGE, CE_ADD_TAG:NOT_LIVING:OPPOSED_TO_LIFE:NO_EAT:NO_SLEEP:..., CE_REMOVE_TAG, CE_DISPLAY_TILE/CE_FLASH_TILE (renders the effect), CE_CAN_DO_INTERACTION (grants new castable powers -> nested [CDI:INTERACTION:...:TARGET:A:LINE_OF_SIGHT:WAIT_PERIOD:10]) — all keyed on START/PEAK/END tick offsets. Secrets add learnability metadata (IS_SECRET_GOAL:IMMORTALITY, MUNDANE_RECORDING_POSSIBLE:objects/text/secret_death.txt).
FLOW: CREATURE_ACTION interactions the unit CAN_DO_INTERACTION are AI-selected in play (spit, pet, necromancer raising corpses) subject to WAIT_PERIOD cooldowns and target predicates. Environmental interactions (SECRET learned from a slab/book, REGION evil clouds, DISTURBANCE from a defiled tomb) attach syndromes automatically. On application the engine schedules each CE_ token at its START/PEAK/END; syndromes can be transmissible (SYN_INGESTED/INHALED/CONTACT) and diluted (creature SYNDROME_DILUTION_FACTOR). Poison, disease, transformation (werebeast), curses (vampirism), and necromancy are ALL the same syndrome mechanism.
FILES: raw/objects/interaction_standard.txt; raw/interaction examples/*.txt; raw/objects/text/secret_death.txt, book_instruction.txt; data/init/world_gen.txt (SECRET_NUMBER, *_NUMBER threat seeds)
LESSONS: ROBUST: a SINGLE predicate+effect+timed-syndrome engine unifies poison, magic, disease, and undeath — new powers are pure data, and because effects are tag add/remove on the universal creature struct, a necromancer that grants NOT_LIVING+NO_EAT and a poison that drops STRENGTH share one executor. Nesting CDI inside CE_CAN_DO_INTERACTION gives recursive, composable spellbooks. FRAGILE: the deeply nested grammar (interaction->effect->syndrome->CE_CAN_DO_INTERACTION->CDI->interaction) is very hard to author and debug; predicate mistakes silently make powers untargetable; transmissible syndromes can produce runaway world-gen plagues.

## Language & Procedural Naming System [data (schema) -> engine (name generator)]
DATA: Three coordinated files per language. raw/objects/language_words.txt (386 KB) is the semantic dictionary: [WORD:AXE] with typed grammatical forms [NOUN:axe:axes]+[FRONT_COMPOUND_NOUN_SING]/[THE_NOUN_SING]/[OF_NOUN_PLUR], [ADJ:ace]+[ADJ_DIST:1], [VERB:act:acts:acted:acted:acting]+[STANDARD_VERB], [PREFIX:after]. raw/objects/language_<RACE>.txt gives each race a [TRANSLATION:DWARF] mapping every WORD to a conlang lexeme ([T_WORD:AXE:libash], [T_WORD:ALE:mabdug]). raw/objects/language_SYM.txt groups words into thematic [SYMBOL:FLOWERY]/[SYMBOL:VIOLENT]/[SYMBOL:EARTH] pools ([S_WORD:BLOSSOM]) that entities SELECT/CULL from for naming styles.
FLOW: At name-generation time the engine picks WORD tokens (constrained by an entity's SELECT_SYMBOL/CULL_SYMBOL pools and value-appropriate themes), assembles them using the grammatical-form flags into compound names (e.g. Front-compound-noun + Rear-compound-noun -> 'Libashanam'), then RENDERS them through the race's TRANSLATION table so dwarves, elves, humans, goblins each speak a distinct-sounding conlang while sharing one underlying semantic name. Applies to person names, site names, artifact/book titles, and entity symbols. Same tables feed generated poetry/book content.
FILES: raw/objects/language_words.txt; raw/objects/language_DWARF/ELF/GOBLIN/HUMAN.txt; raw/objects/language_SYM.txt; raw/objects/text/book_art.txt
LESSONS: ROBUST: separating SEMANTICS (language_words), PHONOLOGY (per-race translation), and STYLE (SYM pools) is a clean three-axis factoring — one name concept renders into any race's tongue, and cultural naming taste (violent vs flowery) is just a pool filter tied to entity values. Grammatical-form flags let compounds be built correctly without hardcoded grammar. FRAGILE: every race needs a full parallel T_WORD table (~50 KB each) kept in sync with the master word list; a missing translation falls back oddly, and the SELECT/SUBSELECT/CULL symbol grammar interacts with entity data in ways hard to trace.

## Reaction / Crafting & Economy System [data (schema) -> sim (item flow)]
DATA: raw/objects/reaction_smelter.txt / reaction_other.txt (46 reactions) / reaction_adv_carpenter.txt. A [REACTION:BRONZE_MAKING] declares [NAME:...] (menu label), [BUILDING:SMELTER:NONE] (station), inputs [REAGENT:A:1:METAL_ORE:TIN]/[REAGENT:B:1:METAL_ORE:COPPER] (id:quantity:item-type:material-filter), outputs [PRODUCT:100:8:BAR:NO_SUBTYPE:METAL:BRONZE]+[PRODUCT_DIMENSION:150] (probability:qty:item:material), a [FUEL] requirement, and [SKILL:SMELT]. Reagents can filter by REACTION_CLASS (e.g. material_template's REACTION_CLASS:CAN_GLAZE) or MATERIAL_REACTION_PRODUCT (plant STRUCTURAL -> DRINK_MAT/SEED_MAT). Item VALUE and MATERIAL_VALUE multipliers, plus quality levels and improvements (entity ITEM_IMPROVEMENT_MODIFIER:BANDS:384), drive the price model; currency is entity [CURRENCY:...].
FLOW: A reaction becomes an available JOB at its [BUILDING]; the job system (above) reserves REAGENTs, consumes FUEL, runs it over the SKILL-scaled tick cost, rolls each PRODUCT's probability, and emits items whose material is resolved from the reagent (material_reaction_product chains). Item value = material_value x item_value x quality x improvements, read by trade/mandate/wealth systems. Wealth totals feed worldgen-style attack triggers and noble mandates. Trade caravans (entity ACTIVE_SEASON) and the [ECONOMY:YES] toggle (d_init) gate the money economy.
FILES: raw/objects/reaction_smelter.txt, reaction_other.txt, reaction_adv_carpenter.txt; raw/objects/item_*.txt; raw/objects/plant_*.txt, inorganic_*.txt; data/init/d_init.txt (ECONOMY); data/help/menu_status_currency/prices/wages, accounts
LESSONS: ROBUST: reactions are declarative graph edges (reagent-filter -> product) so the entire industry tree is data; material-reaction-product chains let one plant/ore flow through many transforms while the engine tracks material identity end-to-end, giving a coherent economy with one item struct. Probability + PRODUCT_DIMENSION model yield/waste uniformly. FRAGILE: reagent MATCHING by type+material filter is stringly-typed — a filter typo yields an unmakeable reaction with no author-time error; value emerges from multiplied coefficients so economy balance is delicate and the [ECONOMY] system was historically buggy enough to be toggle-guarded.

## Crime, Law & Justice System [sim (positions + ethics + off-screen villainy)]
DATA: Two pillars. (a) The GOVERNANCE hierarchy: entity [POSITION:...] blocks with [RESPONSIBILITY:LAW_MAKING/LAW_ENFORCEMENT/MILITARY_STRATEGY], [PRECEDENCE:n] (rank), [NUMBER:1/AS_NEEDED], [APPOINTED_BY:MAYOR/EXPEDITION_LEADER], [COMMANDER:LIEUTENANT:ALL], enforcement squads ([SQUAD:10:fortress guard:...]), and flags SPECIAL_BURIAL/DUTY_BOUND/MENIAL_WORK_EXEMPTION/DO_NOT_CULL/RULES_FROM_LOCATION. Key law roles: MONARCH (LAW_MAKING), SHERIFF & CAPTAIN_OF_THE_GUARD (LAW_ENFORCEMENT). (b) The CRIME MODEL: the entity [ETHIC:...] table classifies acts (THEFT:PUNISH_SERIOUS, TREASON/OATH_BREAKING:PUNISH_CAPITAL, VANDALISM/TRESPASSING/ASSAULT) into punishment tiers. Release notes document a full villainy layer: theft, sabotage, abduction, assassination, coups, embezzlement, framing, with interrogation/counterintelligence and a witness/report model.
FLOW: Mandates and laws flow from LAW_MAKING nobles; violations (missed mandate, murder, theft) are logged as crimes weighted by the ETHIC tier; LAW_ENFORCEMENT positions (sheriff/captain) dispatch the fortress-guard squad to arrest/beat/jail per the punishment tier (hammerings, chains, prison). Off-map, historical figures commit plots the player uncovers via interrogation -> report indicator ('r') -> counterintelligence screen accumulating names/master-links. Justice requires an appointed enforcer to exist ('make sure you have a sheriff to receive the reports').
FILES: raw/objects/entity_default.txt (POSITION + ETHIC blocks); data/help/menu_status_justice, menu_nobles, menu_squads, menu_military; release notes.txt (villainy/interrogation)
LESSONS: ROBUST: crime severity, punishment, diplomacy, and personal thoughts all read the SAME entity ETHIC/VALUE tables, so 'what is a crime and how bad' is one consistent numeric source across scales (world history, fort, individual). The POSITION schema (precedence + responsibility + appointed_by + commander tree) is a general org-chart DSL reused for military, nobility, and religion. FRAGILE: justice depends on an appointed role existing and on the guard squad being able to path/act — if unfilled, crimes go unpunished silently; the villainy/interrogation layer is new and complex, and loyalty edge-cases caused civil-war 'loyalty cascade' bugs cited in release notes.

## Save / Load & Persistence [engine (serializer) + data (frozen raws)]
DATA: State is a per-world region folder under data/save/<region> (absent in this fresh distribution). A save bundles: the frozen COPY of the raws that generated the world (so the type schema travels with the world), the 3D map voxel state, all historical figures/events (the legends XML log), unit/item instances, and civ state. init.txt [COMPRESSED_SAVES:YES] zlib-compresses the streams (the binary dipscript/index files in data/ are already zlib blobs — non-text engine data). d_init autosave knobs: [AUTOSAVE:NONE/SEASONAL/YEARLY], [AUTOBACKUP], [INITIAL_SAVE], [PAUSE_ON_LOAD:YES].
FLOW: Written at worldgen (region export), at autosave cadence, and on retire/quit. On load the engine reads the SAVED raws (not the current install's), reconstructs the type registry, then deserializes instances against it. Backing up = copy the region folder; the readme forbids overwriting old folders (residual files corrupt). Version compat is bounded: 0.47 loads saves from 0.40.03+.
FILES: data/save/ (region folders, output); data/init/init.txt (COMPRESSED_SAVES); data/init/d_init.txt (AUTOSAVE/AUTOBACKUP); release notes.txt (save handling); data/index, data/dipscript/* (zlib engine blobs)
LESSONS: ROBUST: freezing the raws INTO the save is the linchpin of the whole architecture — it decouples the mutable install from immutable worlds, so a modder can change raws for the NEXT world without breaking existing ones, and a shared save carries its own schema. Compression + folder-per-world keeps saves portable. FRAGILE: the same freeze makes schema migration brittle — removing a raw object type from a save's frozen set corrupts it (explicit readme warning), body-config changes destabilize saves, and release notes repeatedly cite save-corruption/duplication bugs (fort/adv retirement, creature/item dupes). No transactional write is data-visible, so a crash mid-save can destroy a world — hence the emphatic 'DO back up saves'.

## Data -> Rendering Pipeline (channels, coordinates, tilesets) [presentation]
DATA: The renderer maps the 3D sim grid to a 2D glyph grid. COORDINATES: world tiles (x,y) x Z-levels (worldgen [LEVELS_ABOVE_GROUND:15], CAVERN_LAYER_COUNT, HAVE_BOTTOM_LAYER); only ONE Z-slice draws at a time onto an [GRID:80:25] character grid (init.txt). CHANNELS per rendered cell: a tile INDEX (0-255 into the tileset) + a COLOR triple (foreground:background:bright-bit), sourced from many token layers — creature [CREATURE_TILE:1][COLOR:3:0:0]/[CREATURE_SOLDIER_TILE:2], material [DISPLAY_COLOR:7:0:0]/[STATE_COLOR:ALL_SOLID:GRAY], syndrome CE_DISPLAY_TILE/CE_FLASH_TILE, and hundreds of terrain-glyph assignments in d_init ([TRACK_NS:186I], [TREE_TRUNK_NS:186], [WOUND_COLOR_BROKEN:4:0:1], [SKY:178:3:0:0], [CHASM]). The palette RGB is data/init/colors.txt (16 named colors x R/G/B). TILESETS are data/art/curses_*.bmp (640x300, 800x600, square 16x16) selected by init.txt [FONT]/[FULLFONT]/[GRAPHICS_FONT]. Optional sprite mode: raw/graphics/graphics_example.txt binds [TILE_PAGE:DWARVES][FILE:...][TILE_DIM:16:16] and [CREATURE_GRAPHICS:DWARF][DEFAULT:DWARVES:0:0:ADD_COLOR][MINER:DWARVES:1:0:AS_IS] mapping (profession/state -> sprite cell + color mode).
FLOW: Per render frame (throttled to init.txt [G_FPS_CAP:50], independent of sim FPS_CAP): the engine takes the current Z-slice, and for each of the 80x25 cells resolves the top visible object (creature > item > building > terrain) to its (tile,fg,bg,bright) via the token precedence, applies FLASH tokens on their FREQUENCY cadence, blits the tileset BMP glyph at that index tinted by the colors.txt palette. [PARTIAL_PRINT] redraws only dirty cells; [BLACK_SPACE], [TEXTURE_PARAM:NEAREST], [PRINT_MODE] (SDL/OpenGL) control the blit. In sprite mode, CREATURE_GRAPHICS overrides the ASCII tile with the mapped sprite cell.
FILES: data/init/init.txt (GRID/FONT/PRINT_MODE/G_FPS_CAP); data/init/d_init.txt (TRACK_*/TREE_*/WOUND_COLOR_*/SKY/CHASM tile assignments); data/init/colors.txt; data/art/curses_*.bmp; raw/graphics/graphics_example.txt; raw/objects/*.txt (per-object *_TILE/COLOR/DISPLAY_COLOR)
LESSONS: ROBUST: the ASCII channel model (index + fg:bg:bright) is a tiny, uniform interface — EVERY object, from magma to a necromancer's flash, emits the same 4 numbers, so the renderer is a single dumb blitter and the tileset is hot-swappable without touching data. Decoupling G_FPS_CAP from sim FPS keeps display smooth under sim load. Externalizing terrain glyphs to d_init lets players re-skin tracks/trees/wounds without a new binary. FRAGILE: only one Z-level renders at once — the 3D world is perceived through a 2D keyhole, a fundamental UX cost; glyph precedence is engine-fixed (modders can recolor but not reorder what wins a cell); and the ASCII-first design meant true graphics were a bolt-on (this legacy build predates the Steam graphical rewrite the release notes announce is 'a few weeks' away).

## Announcement / Event Notification Channel [engine (event bus) + data (routing table)]
DATA: data/init/announcements.txt is a routing table of ~339 event types, each a line [EVENT_NAME:FLAG:FLAG:...]. Flags control per-game-mode handling: A_D (adventure-mode display), D_D (dwarf/fortress display), BOX (open a centered pop-up + implicitly pause), P (pause), R (recenter camera on the event), UCR (unit-combat-report routing), etc. Categories span discovery (FEATURE_DISCOVERY, STRUCK_DEEP_METAL, REACHED_PEAK), the whole ~200-entry COMBAT_* family (COMBAT_DODGE, COMBAT_PARRY, COMBAT_WRESTLE_CHOKEHOLD, COMBAT_GRAB_TEAR), and lifecycle/endgame (ERA_CHANGE, ENDGAME_EVENT_1/2). Human-readable announcement TEXT lives in data/announcement/* (fortressintro, kingarrival, merchantintro, diplomatrebuffed, end2..end5).
FLOW: Simulation subsystems (combat solver, mining, migration, diplomacy, justice) EMIT typed events into this bus during a tick; the router looks each type up in announcements.txt and applies its flags for the current mode — appending to the scrolling log, opening a BOX (force-pausing the sim loop), recentering the view (R), and/or filing it into the combat/unit report archives read with 'r'. This is the primary decoupling seam between sim and UI: producers just name an event, the table decides presentation.
FILES: data/init/announcements.txt (routing + flags); data/announcement/* (fortressintro, kingarrival, merchantintro, diplomatrebuffed, end2-5, unretire); data/help/text_viewer*
LESSONS: ROBUST: a data-driven pub/sub table is the cleanest inter-layer contract in the codebase — any sim subsystem raises an event by NAME and the player fully controls (per mode) whether it pauses, recenters, boxes, or is ignored, without recompilation; this is how DF tames an overwhelming event firehose. Combat generating its own ~200 granular event types means the blow-by-blow combat log is emergent from the same channel. FRAGILE: it is a flat global table with no severity/priority beyond the flags, so tuning which events pause the game is a hand-maintained balancing act; a mis-flagged event either spams the log or force-pauses constantly. Localization/text lives in a separate data/announcement tree that must stay in sync with the type names.

## Configuration / Init Layer [presentation + engine (boot config)]
DATA: data/init/ is the boot-time control surface, read every launch (unlike raws, not frozen into saves). init.txt: display/runtime — [WINDOWED], [FONT]/[FULLFONT], [GRID:80:25], [PRINT_MODE]-equivalents, [FPS]/[FPS_CAP:100]/[G_FPS_CAP:50], [VSYNC], [MOUSE], [KEY_HOLD_MS:150], [SOUND]/[VOLUME], [COMPRESSED_SAVES], [INTRO]. d_init.txt: gameplay tunables + tile assignments — feature toggles [TEMPERATURE]/[WEATHER]/[ECONOMY]/[INVADERS]/[CAVEINS]/[ARTIFACTS], [AUTOSAVE], [PATH_COST], [STORE_DIST_*], [EMBARK_RECTANGLE:4:4], and the WOUND_COLOR/TRACK/TREE/SKY glyph table. interface.txt: the full input map — [DISPLAY_STRING:...] key names and [BIND]/[KEY] chords (keyboard-first UI). colors.txt: 16-color RGB palette. arena.txt: object-test-arena defaults. world_gen.txt: gen presets (see Worldgen).
FLOW: Parsed once at process start into engine globals; changes require a restart (some via in-game settings screens that rewrite the files). interface.txt bindings are resolved every input poll to translate raw SDL keycodes into game actions before the sim/UI consumes them. Toggles in d_init gate whole subsystems at load (e.g. [ECONOMY:YES] enables the money layer; [TEMPERATURE:NO] disables the heat solver for FPS).
FILES: data/init/init.txt, d_init.txt, interface.txt, colors.txt, arena.txt, announcements.txt, world_gen.txt; file changes.txt (per-version init/key deltas)
LESSONS: ROBUST: cleanly separating BOOT config (data/init, mutable, per-install) from WORLD schema (raw, frozen per-world) means players retune display/keys/FPS/toggles freely without touching or invalidating saves. Subsystem toggles let players trade simulation depth for performance (disable temperature/weather) — resilience through optionality. interface.txt externalizing every keybinding makes the dense keyboard UI fully remappable. FRAGILE: the config surface is large and positional; toggles like [ECONOMY] existed partly because those subsystems were buggy enough to need an off-switch; no schema validation means a malformed init line degrades silently to defaults.

## ARCHITECTURE SUMMARY
Mountaincore (formerly King under the Mountain) is a Java 17 / LibGDX colony simulation. Its layering philosophy is a strict separation between an immutable-at-startup DATA layer, a mutable SIM layer holding all per-game state, and a stateless PRESENTATION layer that reads the sim each frame. Nothing in the sim knows about rendering; renderers hold no game state.

LAYERING. (1) DATA/definitions: JSON under core/mods/base and core/assets/definitions is parsed at boot into ~70 singleton *Dictionary classes (ItemTypeDictionary, GoalDictionary, RaceDictionary, JobTypeDictionary, GameMaterialDictionary, etc.). These are read-only lookups shared by every game. Modding is first-class: mods are layered folders that overwrite/extend base JSON. (2) SIM: a single GameContext bean holds ALL per-game state — ConcurrentHashMaps of entities/jobs/rooms/constructions/squads, the TiledMap, GameClock, Random(seeded RandomXS128), SettlementState, and a job-request queue. (3) PRESENTATION: renderers + LibGDX Scene2D UI that read GameContext.

DEPENDENCY INJECTION & AUTO-WIRING. Guice builds the object graph once (MountaincoreGuiceModule). At boot the app uses org.reflections to discover every implementor of three marker interfaces and register them: Updatable (needs per-frame tick), GameContextAware (needs to reset when a game starts/loads), DisplaysText (rebuild on language change), AssetDisposable (free GPU assets). This reflection-based registry is the spine — a new system just implements the right interface and is automatically ticked and lifecycle-managed, with a duplicate-singleton guard.

COMMUNICATION. Systems are decoupled through a single event bus: a customized LibGDX MessageDispatcher (ThreadSafeMessageDispatcher) carrying ~277 integer MessageType codes. Telegraphs subscribe by code; dispatch is immediate or time-delayed via a PriorityBlockingQueue keyed on GdxAI Timepiece. Pending telegrams are themselves persisted in saves. This is how combat, jobs, construction, notifications, status effects, and UI all talk without direct references. Direct method calls are reserved for tight hot loops (entity component ticks, rendering).

THE LOOP & CADENCES. MountaincoreApplicationAdapter.render() clamps delta to a 15fps floor, runs Steam callbacks, drains the message dispatcher, renders the current screen, then audio/twitch/background tasks. MainGameScreen.updateGameLogic multiplies delta by GameClock.speedMultiplier (game speed) and calls GameUpdateRegister.update(delta, isPaused), which ticks every Updatable that either runsWhilePaused or when unpaused. Cadence is deliberately staggered to spread CPU: entities split into update-every-frame (steering, active behaviour) vs infrequent (a rolling cursor updates the whole population every ~3s — needs, goal re-planning); many managers self-throttle with a timeSinceLastUpdate accumulator (JobStore 0.141s, BackgroundTaskManager ~1.05s); the GameClock emits HOUR_ELAPSED/DAY_ELAPSED/YEAR_ELAPSED events that drive slow systems (immigration, trading, weather, invasions) off wall-clock rather than per-frame polling.

CONSISTENCY GUARANTEES. State lives in exactly one place (GameContext); GameContextRegister.setNewContext first calls clearContextRelatedState() on every registered system, then onContextChange() — so loading a save or starting a new game is a clean two-phase reset with no stale references. Persistence is a hand-rolled Persistable/ChildPersistable contract: every stateful object serializes to FastJSON and cross-references others by ID (jobs by jobId, entities by entityId, allocations by id), with a SavedGameStateHolder acting as the id→object resolver on load. Multithreading is confined: pathfinding and other heavy work run on a fixed thread pool (BackgroundTaskManager); the map and its tiles use concurrent collections and are read-only from worker threads, with results delivered back onto the main thread as messages. Coordinates use one convention throughout — world-space Vector2 (1 unit = 1 tile) for entities, floor(pos) → GridPoint2 for tiles — so data and rendering never disagree on where something is.

FRAGILITY NOTES. The GOAP deadlock detector (counting repeated action/outcome pairs) and comments like "FIXME No idea how this is sometimes being null" reveal the cost of a highly emergent, message-driven design: ordering and lifecycle bugs are the recurring hazard. There is no traditional RPG dialogue or crime/law system — social output is Notifications + i18n text; "AI" is colony GOAP, not conversational.

## ARCHITECTURE SUMMARY
Daggerfall Unity is a faithful re-implementation of a 1996 DOS RPG on Unity, and its architecture is organized around one idea: regenerate everything deterministic, persist only the deltas, and let a single integer clock drive all simulation. The code is a single assembly but rigorously layered by namespace into three tiers. (1) DaggerfallConnect — a Unity-free data/API layer of pure C# readers over the classic ARENA2 binary archives (MapsFile, BlocksFile, WoodsFile, Arch3dFile…) plus value structs (DFLocation, DFBlock, DFMesh) and the authoritative coordinate math. (2) DaggerfallWorkshop (the 'engine') — content services and the presentation bridge: the DaggerfallUnity singleton (asset readers, WorldTime, swappable ITerrainSampler/ITextProvider/ITerrainTexturing), StreamingWorld + terrain generation, settlement/dungeon layout, and the rendering skin (meshes, atlased materials, billboards, sky, lighting). (3) DaggerfallWorkshop.Game (the 'sim') — the live session under a second singleton, GameManager: entities, AI, magic, quests, dialogue, items/economy, crime, and save/load.

Systems communicate through three disciplined channels rather than direct coupling. First, two hub singletons: DaggerfallUnity.Instance for engine/content, GameManager.Instance for the running game — everything reaches shared state through these cached references. Second, a broadcast event bus for milestones (WorldTime fires OnDawn/OnNewDay/…, EntityEffectBroker fires OnNewMagicRound, PlayerEnterExit fires transition events, StreamingWorld fires init/promote events), with a deliberate design rule — documented in WorldTime.cs — that high-population consumers (lights, billboards) should poll WorldTime.Now each frame instead of subscribing, because event fan-out breaks down at high timescales. Third, stable data keys instead of object references: buildingKey, door recordIndex, item UID, ulong serializable IDs, and region indices let systems refer to things that may not currently be instantiated (a shop in an unstreamed town, an item in a closed container, an enemy across a save).

What keeps it all consistent is the treatment of time and determinism. Two cadences coexist: GameManager.ClassicUpdate, a 1/16-second flag that reproduces the original's framerate-coupled tick so AI feels identical at any fps (and even reuses classic DFRandom formulas); and the game-minute clock (DaggerfallDateTime.ToClassicDaggerfallTime()), a monotonic integer that every slow system iterates over. PlayerEntity.Update is the heartbeat — it replays every skipped minute in a catch-up loop, firing fatigue, prices, reputation normalization, faction/region updates, disease, and enemy spawns at their modulo-day cadences, so rest, fast travel, and jail all 'simulate the gap' losslessly; the magic and effect systems catch up the same way behind a bounded cap. Because the world (terrain heights, town blocks, fixed dungeon geometry) is a pure function of seed data — computed off-thread in Burst-compiled jobs over NativeArrays and 'promoted' to Unity in one atomic step, then handed to the GPU as channel-packed tilemap textures — saves need store only player-authored state and stateful object deltas via versioned JSON. Two explicit consistency guards protect this: the ITerrainSampler.Version stamped into saves (so a changed height algorithm can't drop the player through the floor) and the floating-origin reset keyed to map-pixel boundaries (bounding float precision while matching how saves store position relative to the central pixel). The recurring structural motifs — data-driven templates with tiny per-instance deltas, interface seams at every generation step for mods, perceive/decide/act splits in AI, and 'regenerate the deterministic, persist the authored' — are what make a faithful recreation of a sprawling 1996 world both moddable and robust.

## DaggerfallConnect — file/data API layer [engine (data)]
DATA: Static readers over classic Daggerfall's ARENA2 binary archives, each a class in Assets/Scripts/API: BsaFile (generic BSA container), MapsFile (MAPS.BSA → DFRegion/DFLocation/MapSummary; world is 1000x500 map pixels), BlocksFile (BLOCKS.BSA → DFBlock with RmbBlock/RdbBlock subrecords), WoodsFile (WOODS.WLD small+large heightmaps), Arch3dFile (ARCH3D.BSA → DFMesh), TextureFile/ImgFile/CifRciFile (image archives → DFBitmap), MonsterFile/ItemsFile/FactionFile/ClassFile (game data tables). Pure value structs: DFPosition, DFLocation (with LocationExterior/LocationDungeon/ClimateSettings), DFRegion, DFBlock, DFMesh, DFBitmap. FileProxy wraps memory-mapped or buffered file access.
FLOW: Stateless, read-only, request-driven. Nothing ticks. Readers are constructed once at startup (validated Arena2 path) and cached inside ContentReader. Consumers call e.g. MapFileReader.GetLocation(region,loc) or BlockFileReader.GetBlock(name) on demand during world streaming.
FILES: Assets/Scripts/API/MapsFile.cs, BlocksFile.cs, WoodsFile.cs, Arch3dFile.cs, DFLocation.cs, DFRegion.cs, DFBlock.cs, DFMesh.cs, FileProxy.cs, BsaFile.cs
LESSONS: Clean separation of 'read classic bytes' from 'build Unity objects' — this layer has zero Unity dependencies (plain C#, DaggerfallConnect namespace), which makes it testable and reusable and keeps the presentation engine ignorant of file formats. Fragility: buffers are mutated in place for runtime fixups (e.g. climate dilation clones and overwrites ClimateFile.Buffer), which is fast but means global mutable state that must be reset carefully.

## Game loop, clock & cadence system [sim]
DATA: GameManager singleton caches every subsystem reference (playerEntity, streamingWorld, questMachine, saveLoadManager, etc.). Two cadence primitives: (1) classicUpdate — a bool gated by classicUpdateTimer against classicUpdateInterval=0.0625f (1/16s), approximating DOS Daggerfall's framerate-coupled update tick; (2) game-minute time from WorldTime.DaggerfallDateTime. DaggerfallDateTime is a [Serializable] value class with fixed calendar (30-day months, 12 months, 24h) and constants (SecondsPerDay etc.); default TimeScale=12 so 1 real second ≈ 12 game seconds.
FLOW: GameManager.FixedUpdate() sets the classicUpdate flag; GameManager.Update() polls InputManager and posts UI messages. WorldTime.Update() calls DaggerfallDateTime.RaiseTime(deltaTime*TimeScale) then RaiseEvents() firing OnDawn/OnDusk/OnNewHour/OnNewDay/OnNewMonth/OnNewYear by comparing last* trackers. Systems choose a cadence: real-time-per-frame (motors), classicUpdate (AI, at ~16Hz regardless of fps), per-game-minute (needs/economy/magic rounds), or event subscription (lights, weather).
FILES: Assets/Scripts/Game/GameManager.cs, Assets/Scripts/Internal/WorldTime.cs, Assets/Scripts/Utility/DaggerfallDateTime.cs, Assets/Scripts/Game/StateManager.cs
LESSONS: The classicUpdate flag is the keystone that decouples AI timing from render framerate while staying faithful to the original's cadence — every EnemyMotor/EnemySenses/EnemyAttack guards work behind `if (GameManager.ClassicUpdate)`, so behavior is identical at 30 or 300 fps. Time is the universal simulation driver: converting to ToClassicDaggerfallTime() (minutes) gives a monotonic integer clock that every catch-up loop iterates over, which is what makes rest/fast-travel/jail 'simulate the skipped time' cleanly. WorldTime's own doc warns events can be skipped at high timescale — hence heavy systems poll Now directly instead of subscribing.

## World coordinate system & streaming world [engine]
DATA: One global virtual world: 1000x500 map pixels; each pixel = 32768 world units (MapsFile.WorldMapTerrainDim), subdivided into WorldMapTileDim=128 tiles and WorldMapRMBDim=4096 (RMB block). PlayerGPS holds WorldX/WorldZ (int native units, 0..32768000 / 0..16384000) and derives CurrentMapPixel via MapsFile.WorldCoordToMapPixel (x/32768, y=499-z/32768 — note Y flip). PlayerGPS also tracks currentRegion/currentLocation/climate/politic, discoveredLocations dict, and nearbyObjects. StreamingWorld keeps a TerrainDistance grid (default 3 → 7x7) of DaggerfallTerrain tiles keyed by MakeTerrainKey(mapPixelX,mapPixelY), plus loose object lists.
FLOW: StreamingWorld.Update(): reads LocalPlayerGPS.CurrentMapPixel; when it changes → UpdateWorld() places/recycles the TerrainDistance grid, InitPlayerTerrain, StartCoroutine(UpdateTerrains) (async job-based heightmap gen spread over frames), then UpdateLocations() once terrain settles. Each frame it also measures player world-unit movement and, on crossing a map-pixel boundary, FloatingOrigin recenters the scene so the central terrain sits at Unity (0,0,0) — offsetting all world objects and applying worldCompensation. Terrains beyond distance+1 are recycled/destroyed.
FILES: Assets/Scripts/Internal/PlayerGPS.cs, Assets/Scripts/Terrain/StreamingWorld.cs, Assets/Scripts/Utility/FloatingOrigin.cs, Assets/Scripts/API/MapsFile.cs
LESSONS: Two coordinate spaces held rigorously distinct: native Daggerfall world units (integer, huge, authoritative, saved) vs Unity scene floats (recentered every map pixel). The floating-origin reset keyed to map-pixel boundaries (not distance) is the crucial trick — it bounds float precision error AND aligns exactly with how saves store player position relative to the central pixel origin, so serialization stays lossless. Terrain generation is job-ified and coroutine-streamed to avoid frame hitches. Fragility called out in code: dungeons can extend beyond the current map pixel, so StreamingWorld deliberately freezes world-position updates while IsPlayerInsideDungeon to avoid desync.

## Procedural terrain generation (heightmap + tilemap) [engine (presentation-feeding)]
DATA: MapPixelData struct (DaggerfallUnityStructs) is the per-tile work packet: mapPixelX/Y, worldClimate/politic, hasLocation + location indices, and parallel data buffers — heightmapData (NativeArray<float>, flattened, indexed via JobA.Idx), tilemapData (NativeArray<byte>), tileMap (NativeArray<Color32> for the shader), avgMaxHeight, plus managed heightmapSamples[y,x] / tilemapSamples[x,y] copies. ITerrainSampler (DefaultTerrainSampler, Version-stamped) defines HeightmapDimension (129), MaxTerrainHeight=1539, OceanElevation, BeachElevation and scale constants.
FLOW: DaggerfallTerrain.UpdateMapPixelData schedules a Burst-compiled IJobParallelFor (GenerateSamplesJob): bicubic-interpolates the WOODS small heightmap (4x4 samples) for base elevation + large heightmap for feature noise + two-octave GetNoise for ground detail, clamps to ocean, normalizes to 0..1. If hasLocation: CalcAvgMaxHeight job → SetLocationTiles → BlendLocationTerrainJob flattens ground under the town. Then ITerrainTexturing.ScheduleAssignTilesJob computes tile indices (marching-squares over a tileData grid of climate/water/beach) → UpdateTileMapDataJob packs tilemapData into Color32 tileMap. CompleteMapPixelDataUpdate copies native→managed and disposes; PromoteTerrainData applies heights to Unity TerrainData and assigns the tilemap texture + material.
FILES: Assets/Scripts/Terrain/TerrainSampler.cs, DefaultTerrainSampler.cs, DaggerfallTerrain.cs, TerrainHelper.cs, TerrainTexturing.cs, JobHelpers.cs
LESSONS: This is the cleanest DATA→RENDERING seam in the repo. Heights and tiles are computed in unmanaged NativeArrays inside Burst jobs (massively parallel, off the main thread, coroutine-awaited), then 'promoted' to Unity Terrain in one atomic step. The tilemap is delivered to the GPU as a Color32 texture (channel-packed tile indices) that a custom terrain shader dereferences into an atlas — so per-vertex texture selection is a data lookup, not per-triangle material assignment. The ITerrainSampler.Version field is serialized into saves specifically so a changed height algorithm won't drop a loaded player through the floor — an explicit, documented consistency guard. Careful manual NativeArray disposal (nativeArrayList) is the cost of going unmanaged.

Buildings are addressed by a stable buildingKey rather than object reference, which survives streaming/reload and lets quests, talk, and crime reference a shop that may not currently be instantiated. ## Entity model & per-frame simulation heartbeat [sim]
DATA: DaggerfallEntityBehaviour (MonoBehaviour) hosts an abstract DaggerfallEntity (PlayerEntity or EnemyEntity or CivilianEntity). DaggerfallEntity holds DaggerfallStats (8 attributes), DaggerfallSkills, Level, and current/max Health/Fatigue/Magicka plus resistances and a big set of per-frame constant-effect flags (IsParalyzed, IsWaterWalking, MaxMagickaModifier…). PlayerEntity adds RegionDataRecord[62] (LegalRep, prices, war/famine/plague flags), factionData, items (ItemCollection), reputations, career, and lastGameMinutes tracker. EnemyEntity wraps a MobileEnemy template (career index, EntityTypes.EnemyMonster/EnemyClass).
FLOW: DaggerfallEntityBehaviour.Update() calls Entity.Update(this) each frame; FixedUpdate calls Entity.FixedUpdate(). ClearConstantEffects zeroes the flag set every frame — effects must re-assert themselves each magic round (constant-state pattern). PlayerEntity.Update is the game's main simulation loop: it computes gameMinutes = WorldTime.ToClassicDaggerfallTime(), and drives per-minute events (fatigue loss scaled by activity/athleticism, expire magic items), per-day events (UpdateRegionalPrices, SetClimateWeathers, expire rented rooms, LoanChecker), and modulo-day events (NormalizeReputations %112d, RegionPowerAndConditionsUpdate %7d/%38d, racial override quests %84d) by iterating every skipped minute so fast-travel/rest catch up faithfully; it also runs IntermittentEnemySpawn and low-legal-rep guard spawns per skipped minute.
FILES: Assets/Scripts/Game/Entities/DaggerfallEntity.cs, DaggerfallEntityBehaviour.cs, PlayerEntity.cs, EnemyEntity.cs, DaggerfallStats.cs, DaggerfallSkills.cs
LESSONS: The integer game-minute clock + catch-up for-loop is the backbone of the whole simulation: any amount of skipped time is replayed minute-by-minute so no event is lost, at the cost of a bounded loop after huge jumps. 'ClearConstantEffects every frame, re-assert from active effects' is an elegant way to make buff/debuff state self-consistent without bookkeeping which effect removed which flag. Attribute/skill/vital access is virtualized (GetCurrentHealth etc.) so effects and mods can hook. Guard clauses (LoadInProgress, CurrentHealth<=0, gameStarted) protect the loop from firing during load or before the clock is real — the code even throws if lastGameMinutes>gameMinutes to catch clock regressions loudly.

## Enemy AI (senses, motor, attack) [sim]
DATA: Three cooperating MonoBehaviours per enemy: EnemySenses (target, DetectedTarget, distanceToPlayer, predicted target position, wouldBeSpawnedInClassic envelope, LOS/hearing/stealth checks), EnemyMotor (movement, pathing, hostile state, take-action callbacks, melee/ranged/spell decision), EnemyAttack (MeleeTimer, damage application, bow damage). Enemy stats come from EnemyEntity/MobileEnemy.
FLOW: All three run in FixedUpdate but gate real logic behind GameManager.ClassicUpdate (~16Hz). EnemyMotor: reads senses, turns toward target within yaw, moves, and rolls attack chance `DFRandom.rand() % speed >= (speed>>3)+6` when MeleeTimer==0 — the exact classic formula. EnemyAttack applies damage on timer expiry. GiveUpTimer drops pursuit when target lost.
FILES: Assets/Scripts/Game/EnemySenses.cs, EnemyMotor.cs, EnemyAttack.cs, EnemyHealth.cs, EnemyDeath.cs, Assets/Scripts/Utility/EnemyBasics.cs
LESSONS: Faithful re-implementation of classic timing: gating on ClassicUpdate plus reusing classic RNG (DFRandom) and bit-shift attack formulas reproduces original feel deterministically regardless of framerate. Clean role split — Senses = perception (what do I know), Motor = decision+locomotion (what do I do), Attack = execution — lets each be tuned or modded independently and mirrors a perceive/decide/act loop. The 'wouldBeSpawnedInClassic' shadow-computation is a neat compatibility device: DFU renders enemies at full draw distance but still respects classic's spawn envelope for gameplay parity (e.g. whether an enemy would have existed). DisableAI global switch short-circuits all three for debugging/cutscenes.

## Quest system (VM over classic QBN) [sim]
DATA: QuestMachine singleton holds active quests + registered action templates + a factory. ## Items & economy [sim]
DATA: DaggerfallUnityItem: itemGroup (ItemGroups enum) + templateIndex into ITEMS.BSA templates, nativeMaterialValue, value, currentCondition/maxCondition, enchantmentPoints, stackCount, plus a stable UID. ItemCollection wraps an OrderedDictionary keyed by UID (preserves order, prevents dup UIDs) and supports typed custom items. Serialized as ItemData_v1. DaggerfallLootDataTables holds LootChanceMatrix tables per enemy/location for drop generation. Prices live in PlayerEntity.RegionDataRecord (per-region, drifting).
FLOW: Items are created from templates (ItemHelper) or loot tables (matrix roll → GenerateItems). Economy: FormulaHelper.CalculateCost(baseValue, shopQuality, condition) → CalculateTradePrice(selling) applies shop quality and buy/sell spread; CalculateRoomCost for inns. UpdateRegionalPrices(regionData, daysPast) is called from PlayerEntity's per-day tick to drift each region's price index. Loot containers (DaggerfallLoot) hold ItemCollections and are serialized per-scene.
FILES: Assets/Scripts/Game/Items/DaggerfallUnityItem.cs, ItemCollection.cs, ItemHelper.cs, Assets/Scripts/Game/Formulas/FormulaHelper.cs, Assets/Scripts/Internal/DaggerfallLootDataTables.cs, DaggerfallLoot.cs
LESSONS: UID-keyed OrderedDictionary is the load-bearing choice: stable per-item identity survives save/reload and lets quests/enchantments reference a specific item; ordered iteration keeps inventory UI deterministic; dup-UID guard catches serialization bugs early. Item = template index + a few mutable fields keeps items tiny and data-driven (base stats come from the shared template, only deltas are stored). Economy is regional and time-driven — prices are state on the region record, mutated by the same per-day catch-up loop, so a returning player finds a shifted market without any per-shop bookkeeping. Every price formula is override-hookable (TryGetOverride) for mods.

## Crime, law & guards [sim]
DATA: Crimes enum (None, Trespassing, Breaking_And_Entering, Assault, Murder, Tax_Evasion, Criminal_Conspiracy, Pickpocketing, Theft, High_Treason, LoanDefault …) held as PlayerEntity.crimeCommitted. Per-region PlayerEntity.RegionDataRecord.LegalRep (clamped legalRepMin..Max) and SeverePunishmentFlags (banishment) track standing. Guards are enemy entities spawned from NPC guards / city guard markers. Witnessing uses line-of-sight checks (per repo's recent guard/crime-response work).
FLOW: A crime sets crimeCommitted and lowers regional LegalRep. In PlayerEntity.Update's per-minute spawn loop: if regionData[region].LegalRep < -10 → 5% chance/minute to set Criminal_Conspiracy and SpawnCityGuards; if banished (SeverePunishmentFlags&1) → 10% chance. MakeNPCGuardsIntoEnemiesIfGuardsSpawned converts nearby NPC guards to hostiles. guardsArriveCountdown times delayed arrival. Arrest flow offers court (fine/prison/execution); prison advances the clock (triggering catch-up). LegalRep normalizes toward 0 every 112 days.
FILES: Assets/Scripts/Game/Entities/PlayerEntity.cs (Crimes enum, SpawnCityGuards, RegionDataRecord), Assets/Scripts/Game/PlayerActivate.cs, Assets/Scripts/Game/Formulas/FormulaHelper.cs
LESSONS: Law is modeled as slowly-drifting per-region reputation state rather than per-event flags, so consequences accumulate and decay naturally through the same time-catch-up machinery that drives everything else (guards can appear minutes after the crime, and rep heals over months). Reusing the enemy system for guards (NPC→enemy conversion) avoids a separate combat path. Crime is one shared enum consumed by activation, combat, and the court UI — a single source of truth. Fragility: probabilistic per-minute guard spawns mean enforcement is stochastic and depends on time actually advancing, so standing still can dodge response.

## Save / load & serialization [engine]
DATA: SaveLoadManager writes a save folder per slot containing separate JSON files: SaveData.txt (SaveData_v1: dateAndTime, playerData, escorting faces, climbing state…), SaveInfo.txt (SaveInfo_v1 metadata), FactionData.txt (FactionData_v2), QuestData.txt (QuestMachine.QuestMachineData_v1), DiscoveryData.txt (PlayerGPS discovered locations). Scene/object state is managed by SerializableStateManager, which keeps List<Dictionary<ulong, ISerializableGameObject>> by type (enemies, action doors, action objects, loot containers, player) plus a per-scene sceneDataCache keyed by scene name. Serialization uses fullserializer (Serialize/Deserialize helpers), not BinaryFormatter.
FLOW: Save: BuildSaveData() snapshots date/time, player, and manager states; SerializableStateManager.GetPlayerData and each registered ISerializableGameObject.GetSaveData produce plain data structs; everything is serialized to JSON and written to files (SaveGame coroutine). Objects self-register via SaveLoadManager.RegisterSerializableGameObject on spawn and deregister on destroy. Load: RestoreSaveData restores date/time and player, then scene objects restore from the cache as scenes stream in (RestoreCachedSceneNextFrame). Per-scene caches let transient scenes (interiors/dungeons) round-trip.
FILES: Assets/Scripts/Game/Serialization/SaveLoadManager.cs, SerializableStateManager.cs, SerializableEnemy.cs, SerializablePlayer.cs, SerializableActionDoor.cs, SerializableLootContainer.cs, SerializableGameObject.cs
LESSONS: Versioned data structs (_v1/_v2) + human-readable JSON = forward-compatible, debuggable, mod-friendly saves; splitting into multiple files means a corrupt faction blob doesn't kill the whole save and large sub-systems save independently. The ISerializableGameObject register/deregister pattern turns 'what's in the world' into a live dictionary keyed by stable ulong IDs, so only stateful deltas are stored while all deterministic content (terrain, town layout, fixed dungeon geometry) is regenerated — keeping saves small. The per-scene cache is what makes the parent-swap interior/dungeon model survive reload. Consistency guard: the terrain sampler Version is saved so the world regenerates identically.

## Presentation / rendering bridge (billboards, meshes, materials, sky, weather, audio) [presentation]
DATA: DaggerfallMesh/ModelCombiner turn DFMesh into Unity meshes; MaterialReader/TextureReader/TextureAtlasBuilder turn classic textures into atlased Materials with climate/season swaps; DaggerfallBillboard/DaggerfallBillboardBatch render flats (sprites) with camera-facing quads and animation; MobileUnit animates enemy sprite sheets by view angle. DaggerfallSky renders the classic layered sky; WeatherManager/PlayerWeather drive fog/rain/snow from the climate array; SunlightManager/DaggerfallLight handle time-of-day lighting; SongManager/DaggerfallSongPlayer select music by context. RetroRenderer/RetroPresentation provide 320x200/640x400 palette modes.
FLOW: Mostly event- and poll-driven off WorldTime and PlayerEnterExit: DaggerfallLight reads WorldTime.Now directly each frame to switch lights at LightsOn/Off hours; WeatherManager subscribes to per-day climate updates (SetClimateWeathers, UpdateWeatherFromClimateArray flag set in PlayerEntity's per-day tick); SunlightManager interpolates by hour; billboards align to camera in LateUpdate; SongManager swaps tracks on location/dungeon transitions. Terrain material consumes the generated Color32 tilemap texture (see terrain system) each promote.
FILES: Assets/Scripts/Internal/DaggerfallBillboard.cs, DaggerfallBillboardBatch.cs, DaggerfallMesh.cs, DaggerfallSky.cs, DaggerfallLight.cs, Assets/Scripts/Game/WeatherManager.cs, SunlightManager.cs, SongManager.cs, Assets/Scripts/Utility/RetroRenderer.cs, MaterialReader (Internal)
LESSONS: Rendering is a thin, reactive skin over simulation state — it reads authoritative data (time, climate, tilemap, entity vitals) rather than owning any, so gameplay is fully decoupled from visuals (retro vs modern lighting are just different presenters). Texture atlasing + billboard batching + mesh combining are the performance backbone that lets a 1996 asset set render at huge draw distances. Climate/season material swaps are data-indexed (climate index → archive), echoing the terrain tilemap trick: choose art by a data lookup, not by branching code. The per-object 'poll WorldTime.Now in Update' pattern (documented in WorldTime.cs) is chosen over events precisely because thousands of lights/billboards would overwhelm the event fan-out at high timescales.
